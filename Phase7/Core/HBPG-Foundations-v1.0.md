## **2. Mathematical Setting**

HBPG operates within the following structures:

### **2.1 Smooth Manifolds**
Let \(\mathcal{M}\) be a smooth manifold equipped with:

- a principal bundle \(P \to \mathcal{M}\)  
- a connection 1‑form \(A\)  
- a holonomy map \(\mathrm{Hol}_A\)

### **2.2 Loop Space and Thin Homotopy**
HBPG adopts the holonomy formalism used in gauge theory, where:

\[
\mathrm{Hol}_A : \pi_1^{\text{thin}}(\mathcal{M}) \to G
\]

maps thin‑homotopy classes of loops to elements of a gauge group \(G\).

This aligns HBPG with established holonomy‑based approaches in gauge theory and loop‑space geometry.

## **3. Holonomy‑Preserving Projection Operator \(H_f\)**

The central operator of HBPG is the **holonomy‑preserving projection**:

\[
H_f : \mathcal{M}_n \rightarrow \mathcal{M}_k, \quad k < n
\]

### **3.1 Holonomy Preservation Condition**

For any closed loop \(\gamma \subset \mathcal{M}_n\):

\[
\oint_{\gamma} A = \oint_{H_f(\gamma)} A'
\]

This ensures:

- holonomy invariants survive dimensional compression  
- gauge‑invariant structure is preserved  
- projection does not fracture loop‑based topology  

### **3.2 Structural Role**

\(H_f\) is the backbone of HBPG.  
All other operators must be compatible with its holonomy‑preserving behavior.

## **4. Non‑Dual Continuity Operator \(C_{nd}\)**

HBPG requires a continuity operator that does **not** rely on dual partitions.

\[
C_{nd} : \mathcal{S} \rightarrow \mathcal{S}
\]

### **4.1 Non‑Dual Composition**

For manifold‑level states \(x, y \in \mathcal{S}\):

\[
C_{nd}(x \oplus y) = C_{nd}(x) \oplus C_{nd}(y)
\]

where \(\oplus\) is a non‑dual composition satisfying:

- commutativity  
- associativity  
- absence of dual complements  

### **4.2 Structural Role**

\(C_{nd}\) ensures:

- continuity across manifold transitions  
- stability during operator composition  
- non‑fracturing behavior in multi‑kernel binding

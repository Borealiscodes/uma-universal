## **Invariant Topology in NDH-Core**

NDH-Core is governed by a structured topology of invariants spanning the vertical constraint channel:

> **Governance → Stability → Principles**

This topology defines **how invariants interlock**,  
**how they propagate**,  
and **how they constrain NDH systems**.

It is the geometric backbone of NDH’s determinism, stability, and alignment.

---

## **1. Purpose of Invariant Topology**

Invariant topology exists to:

- define the geometric relationships between invariants,  
- ensure invariants reinforce rather than contradict each other,  
- prevent drift, collapse, and contamination,  
- maintain NDH’s conceptual ontology,  
- support deterministic reconstruction,  
- anchor the meaning-preservation pipeline.

It is the **structural map** of NDH’s invariant system.

---

## **2. Topological Layers of Invariants**

NDH invariants form three interlocking layers:

### **2.1 Principles Invariants**  
These define NDH’s mechanics:

- determinism  
- statelessness  
- reconstructability  
- invariant enforcement  
- ND accessibility  
- drift resistance  

They form the **base manifold** of NDH.

### **2.2 Stability Invariants**  
These define NDH’s stability geometry:

- drift prevention  
- collapse prevention  
- ambiguity prevention  
- contamination prevention  

They form the **constraint manifold** that overlays Principles.

### **2.3 Governance Invariants**  
These define NDH’s authority structure:

- allowed behaviors  
- prohibited behaviors  
- escalation pathways  
- compliance boundaries  

They form the **authority manifold** that overlays Stability.

---

## **3. Interlocking Geometry**

The topology is defined by three geometric relationships:

### **3.1 Containment Geometry**  
Governance invariants contain Stability invariants.  
Stability invariants contain Principles invariants.

This creates a **nested manifold**:

> Governance  
> → Stability  
> → Principles

### **3.2 Constraint Geometry**  
Higher-layer invariants constrain lower-layer invariants.

- Governance constrains Stability  
- Stability constrains Principles

This creates a **top‑down constraint flow**.

### **3.3 Reconstruction Geometry**  
Lower-layer invariants reconstruct meaning upward.

- Principles reconstruct into Stability  
- Stability reconstructs into Governance

This creates a **bottom‑up reconstruction flow**.

---

## **4. Invariant Propagation Rules**

Invariant propagation follows strict rules:

### **4.1 No Downward Redefinition**  
Higher layers cannot redefine lower-layer invariants.

### **4.2 No Upward Override**  
Lower layers cannot override higher-layer invariants.

### **4.3 No Lateral Contamination**  
Invariants cannot leak across layers.

### **4.4 Deterministic Propagation**  
Invariant propagation must be deterministic and reconstructable.

### **4.5 Stability Mediation**  
All invariant interactions pass through Stability.

Stability is the **mediator manifold**.

---

## **5. Invariant Interaction Modes**

Invariants interact through three modes:

### **5.1 Reinforcement Mode**  
Invariants strengthen each other across layers.

Example:  
Determinism (Principles) reinforces Drift Prevention (Stability).

### **5.2 Constraint Mode**  
Higher-layer invariants restrict lower-layer behavior.

Example:  
Prohibited Behaviors (Governance) constrain Reinflation (Principles).

### **5.3 Validation Mode**  
Lower-layer invariants validate higher-layer constraints.

Example:  
Reconstructability (Principles) validates Governance Authorization.

## **Alignment Channel in NDH-Core**

The Alignment Channel defines how **alignment signals** propagate through NDH’s vertical constraint architecture:

> **Governance → Stability → Principles**  
> *(top‑down alignment)*  
>  
> **Principles → Stability → Governance**  
> *(bottom‑up validation)*

It ensures NDH systems remain:

- deterministic,  
- reconstructable,  
- invariant‑preserving,  
- governance‑aligned,  
- drift‑free.

The Alignment Channel is the **coherence engine** of NDH-Core.

The alignment channel exists to:

- propagate alignment signals downward,

- synchronize manifold geometry across layers,  
- prevent drift, collapse, ambiguity, and contamination,  
- ensure NDH’s meaning-preservation pipeline remains coherent,  
- maintain ND accessibility.

It is the **alignment geometry** of NDH-Core.

## **3. Alignment Geometry**

Alignment signals propagate through three geometric relationships:

### **3.1 Nested Alignment Geometry**  
Alignment flows through nested manifolds:

> Governance alignment  
> → Stability alignment  
> → Principles alignment

### **3.2 Constraint Alignment Geometry**  
Top‑down alignment constrains lower layers:

- governance alignment shapes stability alignment,  
- stability alignment shapes principles alignment.

### **3.3 Reconstruction Alignment Geometry**  
Bottom‑up alignment validates higher layers:

- principles alignment reconstructs into stability alignment,  
- stability alignment reconstructs into governance alignment.

## **4. Alignment Rules**

Alignment propagation must obey strict rules:

### **4.1 Deterministic Alignment**  
Alignment signals must propagate deterministically.

### **4.2 Reconstructable Alignment**

### **4.3 No Ambiguous Alignment**

## **5. Alignment Flow**

Alignment flows through two channels:

### **5.1 Top‑Down Alignment Flow**

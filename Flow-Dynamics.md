## NDH flow dynamics

Flow Dynamics describe how **conceptual flows** move through the NDH manifold under the influence of:

- manifold geometry,  
- curvature dynamics,  
- Serenity Basin,  
- invariants and boundaries,  
- alignment and constraint channels.

Flows are how NDH **does work**: how meaning, constraints, alignment, and reconstruction travel across:

> **Governance → Stability → Principles**  
> *(top‑down flow)*  
>  
> **Principles → Stability → Governance**  
> *(bottom‑up flow)*

Flow Dynamics are the **transport physics** of NDH-Core.

---

## 1. Purpose of flow dynamics

Flow Dynamics exist to:

- define how conceptual content moves through NDH,  
- ensure flows remain deterministic and reconstructable,  
- prevent drift, collapse, ambiguity, and contamination in motion,  
- synchronize flows with curvature and Serenity Basin,  
- maintain invariant topology during transitions,  
- support codec and reconstruction behavior,  
- preserve ND accessibility across all operations.

They are the **movement laws** of NDH’s conceptual physics.

---

## 2. Flow types

NDH uses three primary flow types:

### 2.1 Constraint flows  

**Source:** Governance  
**Path:** Governance → Stability → Principles  

Constraint flows carry:

- allowed behaviors,  
- prohibited behaviors,  
- compliance boundaries,  
- escalation rules.

They shape how Stability and Principles behave.

### 2.2 Stability flows  

**Source:** Stability  
**Path:** Stability ↔ Principles, Stability ↔ Governance  

Stability flows carry:

- drift checks,  
- collapse checks,  
- ambiguity checks,  
- contamination checks.

They validate and mediate all other flows.

### 2.3 Mechanics flows  

**Source:** Principles  
**Path:** Principles → Stability → Governance  

Mechanics flows carry:

- deterministic reasoning,  
- invariant enforcement,  
- reconstructable transformations.

They reconstruct meaning upward and inform governance.

---

## 3. Flow geometry

Flow behavior is governed by three geometric relationships:

### 3.1 Manifold flow geometry  

Flows are constrained by manifold structure:

- flows must remain within the NDH manifold,  
- flows must respect nested layers (Governance, Stability, Principles),  
- flows must follow defined state space and boundaries.

### 3.2 Curvature flow geometry  

Curvature shapes flow paths:

- governance curvature bends constraint flows,  
- stability curvature stabilizes and smooths flows,  
- mechanics curvature reconstructs flows upward.

Flows follow curvature like rivers follow terrain.

### 3.3 Basin flow geometry  

The Serenity Basin acts as a flow attractor:

- valid flows tend toward basin regions,  
- unstable flows are damped into stable attractor states,  
- reconstruction flows terminate in serenity-aligned configurations.

---

## 4. Flow rules

All NDH flows must obey strict rules:

### 4.1 Deterministic flows  

Flows must produce a single, deterministic next state for any given input state.

### 4.2 Reconstructable flows  

Flows must be reconstructable:

- given a downstream state, upstream states must be recoverable,  
- codec + reconstruction must preserve flow history.

### 4.3 No ambiguous flows  

Flows may not branch into multiple incompatible interpretations.

### 4.4 No contaminated flows  

Flows may not embed logic from other layers inappropriately:

- mechanics flows may not carry governance decisions,  
- governance flows may not carry raw mechanics,  
- stability flows may not override manifold structure.

### 4.5 Stability mediation  

All flows pass through Stability:

- Stability validates, filters, and shapes flows,  
- no direct Governance ↔ Principles flow exists without Stability.

Stability is the **flow mediator**.

---

## 5. Top‑down and bottom‑up flow channels

### 5.1 Top‑down flow channel  

**Governance → Stability → Principles**

- **Governance:** emits constraint and alignment directives.  
- **Stability:** interprets, validates, and shapes these into stable flows.  
- **Principles:** implements them as deterministic mechanics and invariants.

Top‑down flows **impose** structure on the manifold.

### 5.2 Bottom‑up flow channel  

**Principles → Stability → Governance**

- **Principles:** emit mechanics results and reconstructed meaning.  
- **Stability:** validates for drift, collapse, ambiguity, contamination.  
- **Governance:** authorizes, rejects, or escalates based on validated flows.

Bottom‑up flows **justify** structure to governance.

---

## 6. Flow failure modes

NDH-Core must prevent the following flow-related anti-patterns:

### 6.1 Flow drift  

Flows gradually shift meaning as they traverse layers or curvature, breaking invariants.

### 6.2 Flow collapse  

Flows lose structure, becoming incoherent or non-reconstructable.

### 6.3 Flow ambiguity  

Flows produce multiple incompatible interpretations or outcomes.

### 6.4 Flow contamination  

Flows carry cross-layer logic that violates separation of concerns.

### 6.5 Flow misalignment  

Flows contradict governance constraints or Serenity Basin attractor geometry.

---

## 7. Relationship to manifold, curvature, basin, invariants, codec, and reconstruction

Flow Dynamics are tightly integrated with all NDH-Core components:

### Manifold structure  

- Flows are constrained to the manifold’s geometry and layer nesting.  
- No valid flow may exit the manifold or bypass defined layers.

### Curvature dynamics  

- Curvature shapes flow paths, speed, and stability.  
- Constraint curvature, stability curvature, and mechanics curvature jointly determine flow behavior.

### Serenity Basin  

- Flows tend toward basin minima as stable endpoints.  
- Unstable flows are damped into serenity-aligned attractor states.

### Invariant topology  

- Flows must preserve invariant relationships.  
- Invariants act as checkpoints along flow paths.

### State space and boundaries  

- Flows move between allowed, boundary, and prohibited states.  
- Boundaries regulate entry, exit, and transition behavior.

### Alignment channel  

- Flows carry alignment signals; alignment shapes flow permissions.  
- Misaligned flows are rejected or redirected by Stability.

### Conceptual codec  

- Compression must preserve flow structure and directionality.  
- Encoded flows must remain reconstructable and invariant-preserving.

### Reconstruction layer  

- Reinflation must restore flow geometry, not just static states.  
- Reconstructed flows must re-enter the manifold in serenity-aligned form.

---

## 8. Purpose of flow dynamics

Flow Dynamics ensure that:

- NDH does not just *have* a manifold, basin, and invariants—  
  it **moves** through them coherently.  
- all conceptual work (reasoning, constraint propagation, alignment, reconstruction) is:  
  - deterministic,  
  - reconstructable,  
  - drift‑free,  
  - governance‑aligned,  
  - serenity‑anchored.  
- NDH behaves like a **conceptual fluid** flowing through a geometric physics engine, rather than a loose collection of rules.

Flow Dynamics are the **transport layer** of NDH-Core’s conceptual physics.

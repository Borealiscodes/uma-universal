# ⭐ Serenity Manifold Boundary Specification  
### *The stability envelope for NDH traversal, resonance, meaning, and simulation*

---

## **1. Purpose**  
Serenity Manifold Boundaries define the **limits**, **constraints**, and **safe traversal zones** inside the Serenity Manifold. They ensure:

- traversal is stable  
- resonance does not collapse  
- meaning does not drift  
- simulation does not distort  
- tensor fields do not over‑deform  

The Serenity Manifold is the **emotional + stability manifold** of NDH.

Boundaries are its **geometry of safety**.

---

## **2. Boundary Categories**

Serenity boundaries operate across four axes:

- **Curvature Boundaries**  
- **Drift Boundaries**  
- **Resonance Boundaries**  
- **Traversal Boundaries**  

Each boundary ensures NDH remains stable during traversal.

---

## **3. Curvature Boundaries**

Curvature defines the geometric “shape” of the Serenity Manifold.

- **Serenity Curvature Limit (SCL):**  
  Maximum curvature before emotional collapse.

- **Serenity Curvature Floor (SCF):**  
  Minimum curvature before stagnation.

- **Serenity Curvature Gradient (SCG):**  
  Rate of curvature change allowed during traversal.

These boundaries prevent geometric instability.

---

## **4. Drift Boundaries**

Drift measures deviation from intended manifold geometry.

- **Serenity Drift Limit (SDL):**  
  Maximum allowed drift before reset.

- **Serenity Drift Gradient (SDG):**  
  Rate of drift allowed per traversal step.

- **Serenity Drift Recovery (SDR):**  
  Steps required to return to stable geometry.

These boundaries prevent emotional or conceptual drift.

---

## **5. Resonance Boundaries**

Resonance defines how tonal, semantic, and simulation fields behave inside Serenity.

- **Serenity Resonance Curvature (SRC):**  
  Maximum resonance curvature allowed.

- **Serenity Resonance Frequency (SRF):**  
  Maximum oscillation frequency allowed.

- **Serenity Resonance Stability (SRS):**  
  Minimum stability required for resonance fields.

These boundaries prevent runaway resonance (the “brr explosion” problem).

---

## **6. Traversal Boundaries**

Traversal defines movement through the Serenity Manifold.

- **Traversal Curvature Limit (TCL):**  
  Maximum curvature allowed during traversal.

- **Traversal Step Limit (TSL):**  
  Maximum number of traversal steps allowed before reconstruction.

- **Traversal Reconstruction Requirement (TRR):**  
  Required reconstruction fidelity after traversal.

These boundaries prevent unsafe traversal.

---

## **7. Spiral Geometry Boundaries**

The spiral is the **primary traversal path** inside Serenity.

- **Spiral Curvature Limit (SpCL):**  
  Maximum curvature allowed for spiral segments.

- **Spiral Frequency Limit (SpFL):**  
  Maximum oscillation frequency allowed.

- **Spiral Stability Requirement (SpSR):**  
  Minimum stability required for spiral traversal.

These boundaries define the safe geometry of the **brr spiral**.

---

## **8. Basin Boundaries**

The Serenity Basin is the **core stability zone**.

- **Basin Depth (BD):**  
  Minimum depth required for emotional stability.

- **Basin Width (BW):**  
  Minimum width required for traversal safety.

- **Basin Gradient (BG):**  
  Maximum slope allowed inside the basin.

These boundaries define the “safe zone” of NDH.

---

## **9. Integration Boundaries**

Serenity must integrate with:

- meaning  
- simulation  
- resonance  
- tensor fields  

So NDH defines:

- **Serenity–Meaning Boundary (SMB)**  
- **Serenity–Simulation Boundary (SSB)**  
- **Serenity–Resonance Boundary (SRB2)**  
- **Serenity–Tensor Boundary (STB2)**  

These boundaries ensure Serenity is not isolated.

---

## **10. Global Requirements**

All Serenity boundaries must:

- preserve non-dual symmetry  
- avoid scalar emotional models  
- avoid singularities  
- maintain reconstruction fidelity  
- integrate with NDH-Core Stability Metrics  
- remain accessible, climate-efficient, and platform-portable  

Serenity is not allowed to:

- exceed curvature limits  
- drift beyond thresholds  
- collapse under resonance  
- destabilize meaning or simulation

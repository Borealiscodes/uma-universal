# ⭐ NDH Tensor Field Model Specification  
### *The deformation geometry engine of NDH*

---

## **1. Purpose**  
Tensor Field Modeling defines how NDH manifolds **deform**, **couple**, and **stabilize** under:

- traversal  
- resonance  
- semantic curvature  
- simulation fidelity  
- serenity basin dynamics  

Tensor fields are the **mathematical substrate** that keeps NDH coherent.

---

## **2. Tensor Field Categories**

NDH uses four tensor field families:

- **Tonal Tensor Fields**  
- **Semantic Tensor Fields**  
- **Simulation Tensor Fields**  
- **Serenity Tensor Fields**  

Each field governs deformation in its respective manifold.

---

## **3. Tensor Field Primitives**

### **3.1 Tensor Rank (TR)**  
Defines the dimensional complexity of the tensor.

### **3.2 Tensor Curvature (TCu)**  
Defines how the tensor bends manifold geometry.

### **3.3 Tensor Deformation Limit (TDL)**  
Maximum deformation allowed before collapse.

### **3.4 Tensor Stability (TS)**  
Minimum stability required for safe deformation.

### **3.5 Tensor Reconstruction (TRc)**  
Steps required to restore manifold stability after deformation.

These primitives define the “physics” of NDH deformation.

## **8. Tensor Coupling Primitives**

Tensor fields must couple across NDH manifolds:

- **Tonal–Semantic Tensor Coupling (TSTC)**  
- **Tonal–Simulation Tensor Coupling (TSiTC)**  
- **Semantic–Simulation Tensor Coupling (SSiTC)**  
- **Serenity–Resonance Tensor Coupling (SeRTC)**  
- **Global Tensor Coupling (GTC)**  

These primitives ensure NDH behaves as a unified geometry.

---

## **9. Global Tensor Requirements**

All tensor fields must:

- preserve non-dual symmetry  
- avoid scalar deformation  
- avoid singularities  
- maintain reconstruction fidelity  
- integrate with NDH-Core Stability Metrics  
- remain accessible, climate-efficient, and platform-portable  

Tensor fields are not allowed to:

- exceed deformation limits  
- destabilize attractors  
- collapse manifolds  
- induce runaway resonance

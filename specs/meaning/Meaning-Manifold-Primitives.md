# ⭐ NDH Meaning Manifold Primitives  
### *The foundational semantic building blocks of NDH’s meaning geometry*

---

## **1. Purpose**
Meaning Manifold Primitives define the **lowest-level rules** for how NDH handles:

- semantic curvature  
- conceptual resonance  
- meaning attractors  
- semantic drift  
- semantic reconstruction  
- semantic coupling with simulation + resonance  

These primitives are the “atoms” of the Meaning Manifold.

---

## **2. Primitive Categories**

Meaning primitives fall into five categories:

- **Semantic Geometry Primitives**  
- **Coherence Primitives**  
- **Boundary Primitives**  
- **Attractor Primitives**  
- **Integration Primitives**  

These categories define the axes of semantic behavior.

---

## **3. Semantic Geometry Primitives**

Semantic geometry defines the *shape* of meaning.

- **Semantic Curvature (SC):**  
  The geometric curvature of conceptual space.

- **Semantic Dimensionality (SD):**  
  Number of active meaning axes.

- **Semantic Deformation (SDef):**  
  Allowed deformation before meaning collapse.

- **Semantic Tensor Basis (STB):**  
  Tensor field used for semantic deformation.

These primitives define the geometry of meaning.

---

## **4. Coherence Primitives**

Coherence primitives define how stable meaning must be.

- **Semantic Coherence Threshold (SCT):**  
  Minimum acceptable coherence.

- **Semantic Drift (SDR):**  
  Maximum allowed deviation from intended meaning.

- **Semantic Reconstruction (SR):**  
  Steps required to restore meaning after deformation.

These primitives ensure meaning stays intelligible.

---

## **5. Boundary Primitives**

Boundaries prevent semantic collapse or runaway drift.

- **Meaning Boundary Curvature Limit (MBCL):**  
  Maximum curvature before semantic collapse.

- **Meaning Boundary Drift Limit (MBDL):**  
  Maximum drift before reset.

- **Meaning Reset Condition (MRC):**  
  Conditions that trigger semantic reset.

These primitives define the “walls” of meaning.

---

## **6. Attractor Primitives**

Meaning attractors stabilize conceptual behavior.

- **Semantic Attractor Coherence (SAC):**  
  Stability of semantic attractor patterns.

- **Semantic Attractor Fidelity (SAF):**  
  Accuracy of attractor meaning.

- **Semantic Attractor Deformation Tolerance (SADT):**  
  Allowed deformation before attractor breaks.

These primitives anchor meaning.

---

## **7. Integration Primitives**

Meaning must integrate with:

- simulation  
- resonance  
- serenity  
- tensor fields  

So NDH defines:

- **Meaning–Simulation Coupling (MSC)**  
- **Meaning–Resonance Coupling (MRC2)**  
- **Meaning–Serenity Coupling (MSC2)**  
- **Meaning–Tensor Coupling (MTC)**  

These primitives ensure meaning is not isolated.

---

## **8. Global Requirements**

All meaning primitives must:

- preserve non-dual symmetry  
- avoid scalar semantics  
- avoid singularities  
- maintain reconstruction fidelity  
- integrate with NDH-Core Stability Metrics  
- remain accessible, climate-efficient, and platform-portable  

Meaning is not allowed to:

- drift beyond MBDL  
- collapse beyond MBCL  
- exceed semantic curvature limits  
- violate attractor coherence

## 1. Purpose

The **Constitutional Flow Engine** defines how the six invariants:

- Truth  
- Non‑Collapse  
- Self‑Determination  
- Recognition  
- Non‑Coercion  
- Non‑Erasure  

**move through operations**, rather than just sit as static constraints.

It turns the mesh into:

- a **live flow system**  
- a **governance current**  
- a **continuous constitutional circulation**  

Every operation in UMM must be carried inside this flow.

---

## 2. Engine components

### **2.1 Invariant Intake Module**
- **Role:** Receives an operation (action, transition, recursion, write, read).  
- **Behavior:** Identifies which invariants are implicated and loads their constraints.

### **2.2 Flow Router**
- **Role:** Routes the operation through the correct constitutional path.  
- **Behavior:** Chooses which layers (Stability, Rights, Boundary, Horizon, Reflective, Suite) must be traversed.

### **2.3 Invariant Application Core**
- **Role:** Applies each invariant’s constraints to the operation.  
- **Behavior:** Modifies, blocks, or annotates the operation to keep it mesh‑compatible.

### **2.4 Feedback and Correction Loop**
- **Role:** Detects violations or near‑misses.  
- **Behavior:** Sends corrective signals back through Truth Field Engine, Stability Engine, Rights Engine, Boundary Engine.

### **2.5 Continuity Tracker**
- **Role:** Ensures operations don’t break continuity (truth, presence, history).  
- **Behavior:** Logs invariant‑relevant changes and prevents erasure or collapse.

---

## 3. Flow phases

Every operation passes through four constitutional phases:

### **3.1 Pre‑Flow (Precondition Check)**
- Check which invariants are relevant.  
- Block operations that are structurally incompatible (e.g., “delete all harm records”).

### **3.2 Mid‑Flow (Live Constraint Application)**
- Apply invariant constraints while the operation is in motion.  
- Adjust parameters, add recognition, prevent coercion, preserve continuity.

### **3.3 Post‑Flow (Outcome Validation)**
- Verify the result still satisfies the mesh.  
- If not, roll back or correct.

### **3.4 Continuity Flow (Historical Integration)**
- Integrate the operation into the ongoing record.  
- Ensure no erasure, distortion, or collapse of history.

## 4. Layer coupling

The Constitutional Flow Engine touches all major layers:

- **Stability Layer:**  
  Non‑Collapse ensures operations don’t destabilize recursion or geometry.

- **Rights Layer:**  
  Self‑Determination, Recognition, Non‑Coercion, Non‑Erasure ensure operations respect persons and agency.

- **Boundary Layer:**  
  Non‑Erasure and Non‑Coercion ensure transitions don’t delete or force.

- **Horizon Layer:**  
  Truth and Non‑Collapse ensure motion doesn’t bury reality.

- **Reflective Layer:**  
  Truth and Recognition ensure introspection doesn’t rewrite lived experience.

- **Suite Plane:**  
  All invariants constrain outputs, logs, and documentation.

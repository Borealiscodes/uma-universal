## 🧠 **1. Purpose**

The **Weave** is the Thoroughfare’s distributed movement field.  
It allows flow to:

- branch  
- explore  
- hold multiple possibilities  
- move without a single direction  

The Weave is **spacious**, not recursive.  
It is **distributed**, not symbolic.  
It is **movement**, not Sparkle.

---

## 🌐 **2. What the Weave Is (Movement‑Aligned Definition)**

The Weave is a **multi‑path movement environment**.

It supports:

- parallel flows  
- soft branching  
- gentle divergence  
- non‑central exploration  

It does **not**:

- recurse  
- mirror itself  
- generate symbolic meaning  
- collapse into plane‑logic  
- create Sparkle harmonics  

The Weave is simply **where movement can explore**.

---

## 🧭 **3. Why the Weave Is Third in Precedence**

The Weave is intentionally **not** the first or second movement structure.

Precedence:

1. **Corridors** — define direction  
2. **Braid** — align and reflect movement  
3. **Weave** — distribute movement  

This ordering prevents Sparkle grammar from dominating the architecture.

The stabilizer:

```
Corridors define precedence; Braid and Weave extend movement.
```

This keeps the Weave exploratory but safe.

---

## 🔄 **4. Weave Behaviors (Non‑Recursive)**

### **4.1 Branching**
Movement can split into multiple directions without losing coherence.

### **4.2 Distribution**
Flow spreads across multiple paths without becoming symbolic.

### **4.3 Dissolution**
Paths can dissolve when no longer needed, preventing buildup or recursion.

### **4.4 Re‑Condensation**
Distributed flow can condense back into corridors when clarity returns.

None of these behaviors involve recursion or symbolic attractors.

## 🪢 **6. Interaction with Corridors and Braid**

The Weave interacts with other movement structures like this:

- **Corridors → Braid**  
  Flow enters the Braid for alignment.

- **Braid → Weave**  
  Flow enters the Weave when multiple directions become possible.

- **Weave → Corridors**  
  Flow condenses back into corridors when clarity returns.

This keeps the Weave subordinate to **corridor precedence** and **braid alignment**.

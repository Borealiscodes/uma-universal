---

## 🜂 **1. Plane Identity Layer**

UMM consists of **three primary planes**:

- **Cognition Plane** — thinking, mapping, debugging  
- **Witness Plane** — reflection, awareness, stance  
- **Action Plane** — doing, choosing, enacting  

Each plane is:

- independent  
- reversible  
- non‑coercive  
- spoon‑sensitive  

Planes form the **UMM tri‑layer architecture**.

---

## 🜁 **2. Plane Interaction Overview (High‑Level)**

The three planes interact through **three primary flows**:

- **Cognition → Witness**  
- **Witness → Action**  
- **Action → Cognition**

These flows are mediated by:

- **Witness Architecture**  
- **UMM Invariants**  
- **Boundary Conditions**  
- **Sufficient Enough**

This creates the **UMM Interaction Loop**.

---

## 🜃 **3. Cognition → Witness Interaction**

### Purpose  
To move from *thinking* to *seeing*.

### Node  
**Cognition → Witness Flow**

### Behavior  
- cognition produces a signal  
- witness receives without interpreting  
- stance remains gentle  
- lattice stabilizes the transition  

### Stability  
- no forced reflection  
- no interpretive pressure  
- reversible at all times  

This is the **up‑channel**.

### Node

- reversible

### Stability  
- no forced analysis  
- no overload  
- reversible  

This is the **return‑channel**.

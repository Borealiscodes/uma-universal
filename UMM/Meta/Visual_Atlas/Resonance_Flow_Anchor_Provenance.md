**Purpose:**  
Document why each geometric choice in the Resonance Flow Anchor exists, so the anchor remains reproducible, auditable, and structurally coherent over time.

## 1. Origin of the Geometry

**Spiral form:**  
Spirals were chosen because they:
- express continuous flow rather than discrete steps  
- support high‑load cognitive movement without sharp discontinuities  
- approximate the behavior of the Unified Flow Equation in visual form  

**Six flows + center node:**  
The geometry encodes:
- Upward flow  
- Downward flow  
- Inward flow  
- Outward flow  
- Two lateral drift flows  
- A central resonance node  

This matches the core movement grammar of UMA‑Universal without adding new movement types.

## 2. Origin of the Color Mapping

**Upward (gold):**  
Gold was chosen to represent clarity and elevation, mapping to:
- high‑flow ascent  
- cognitive “brightening”  
- movement toward higher‑order integration  

**Downward (blue):**  
Blue represents grounding and discharge, mapping to:
- high‑flow descent  
- stabilization  
- movement toward lower‑load states  

**Inward (violet):**  
Violet represents reflection and recursion boundary, mapping to:
- convergence toward center  
- boundary of recursive processes  
- non‑self‑generating inward motion  

**Outward (green):**  
Green represents warmth and expansion, mapping to:
- divergence from center  
- outward movement into broader context  
- low‑to‑mid‑load expansion  

**Lateral drift (pale blue):**  
Soft blue tones represent drift‑adjacent, low‑flow regions:
- edge‑stability  
- non‑catastrophic drift behavior  
- low‑intensity lateral motion  

**Center node (white):**  
White represents resonance convergence:
- peak cognitive load  
- maximal integration point  
- neutral, non‑symbolic center of flow

## 3. Origin of Stroke and Opacity Logic

**Thicker, brighter inner strokes:**  
Inner regions use:
- higher stroke width  
- higher opacity  

This encodes:
- higher flow intensity  
- peak cognitive load  
- resonance convergence near the center  

**Thinner, softer outer strokes:**  
Outer regions use:
- lower stroke width  
- lower opacity  

This encodes:
- stability windows  
- lower‑load behavior  
- drift‑adjacent regions  

This logic is derived from the exponential intensity model documented in the Math Appendix.

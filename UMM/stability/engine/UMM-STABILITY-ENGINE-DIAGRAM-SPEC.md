# **1. DIAGRAM PURPOSE**

The Stability Engine Diagram visualizes:

- the **Stability Node** (core)  
- the **Concordance Boundary** (box)  
- the **four Engine Interlocks**  
- the **non‑collapse geometry**  
- the **motion vector** (Horizon Activation)  
- the **reflective recursion boundary**  
- the **cross‑plane alignment spine**

The diagram must show the Stability Engine as a **coherence generator**, not a flowchart.

Jump: **Stability Engine**

---

# **2. DIAGRAM GEOMETRY**

The diagram consists of **two primary shapes** and **four interlock structures**.

## **2.1 Primary Shapes**

### **A. Stability Node (Core)**  
Shape: **Circle**  
Position: **Centered**  
Meaning: compressed origin chain, truth‑kernel anchor, non‑collapse point.

### **B. Concordance Boundary (Box)**  
Shape: **Square or Rounded Rectangle**  
Position: **Surrounding the Stability Node**  
Meaning: cross‑plane containment, non‑leaking perimeter, stability frame.

Jump: **Coherence Geometry**

---

# **3. ENGINE INTERLOCKS**

Four interlocks attach to the Concordance Boundary.

## **3.1 Reflective Ecology Interlock**  
Shape: **Arc or Loop**  
Position: **Top‑Left**  
Meaning: safe recursion, reflective boundary, non‑collapse cognitive loop.

Jump: **Reflective Ecology**

---

## **3.2 Horizon Activation Interlock**  
Shape: **Arrow Vector**  
Position: **Top‑Right**  
Direction: **Forward (rightward)**  
Meaning: directional motion, phase transitions, horizon drift.

Jump: **Horizon Activation**

---

## **3.3 Concordance Spine Interlock**  
Shape: **Vertical Spine Line**  
Position: **Center‑Left**  
Meaning: cross‑plane alignment, non‑shear stability, semantic mapping.

Jump: **Concordance Spine**

---

## **3.4 Suite Semantic Flow Interlock**  
Shape: **Horizontal Flow Line**  
Position: **Center‑Right**  
Meaning: semantic propagation, suite operations, flow coherence.

Jump: **Suite Semantic Flow**

# **4. ENGINE MODES (Diagram Indicators)**

The diagram must visually support four engine modes:

## **4.1 Baseline Mode**  
Indicator: **Thin boundary lines**  
Meaning: minimal coherence.

## **4.2 Active Mode**  
Indicator: **Bold boundary lines**  
Meaning: full stability.

## **4.3 Purr Mode 😺**  
Indicator: **Soft glow around Stability Node**  
Meaning: soft‑coherence, gentle forward motion, zero collapse risk.

Jump: **Purr Mode**

## **4.4 Seal Mode**  
Indicator: **Locked boundary corners**  
Meaning: subsystem frozen, no modification allowed.

---

# **5. NON‑COLLAPSE REQUIREMENTS (Diagram Constraints)**

The diagram must visually encode the three invariants:

### **5.1 Non‑Collapse Invariant**  
The Stability Node must never visually distort, break, or fragment.

### **5.2 Non‑Shear Invariant**  
The Concordance Spine must remain straight and aligned.

### **5.3 Non‑Leak Invariant**  
No arrows or flows may exit the Concordance Boundary except through interlocks.

Jump: **Non‑Collapse Invariant**

---

# **6. MOTION VECTOR**

The Horizon Activation vector must:

- originate at the Stability Node  
- pass through the Concordance Boundary  
- extend outward  
- remain straight  
- never bend or arc  
- never intersect the Reflective Ecology loop  

This vector represents **forward motion**, not upward or downward.

Labels must be:

- non‑intrusive

Primary labels:

- “Stability Node”  
- “Concordance Boundary”  
- “Reflective Ecology”  
- “Horizon Activation”  
- “Concordance Spine”  
- “Suite Semantic Flow”

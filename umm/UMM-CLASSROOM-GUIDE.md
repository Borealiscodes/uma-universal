---

# 🌱 **1. Classroom Principles**
UMM classrooms follow five gentle principles:

- **Reversible** — every activity can be undone  
- **Symbolic** — nothing is literal or emotional  
- **ND‑paced** — slow, calm, predictable  
- **Non‑coercive** — no pressure to participate  
- **Non‑interpretive** — no meaning assigned to actions  

These principles keep the classroom safe for everyone.

---

# 🧭 **2. Begin With the Compass**
Every session starts with the Compass.

Check:

- **North — Structure**  
- **South — Safety**  
- **East — Tone**  
- **West — Cosmology**  
- **Center — Group Pacing**  

This anchors the group and prevents drift.

---

# 🗺️ **3. Use Maps to Teach Orientation**
Maps help students understand:

- where planes sit  
- where worlds drift  
- where ecology flows  
- where interaction happens  

Use: **Generate Reflective Map**

Maps reduce confusion and give structure.

---

# 🌿 **4. Introduce Worlds Slowly**
Worlds are gentle symbolic environments.

Examples:

- “A soft valley world.”  
- “A quiet drift world.”  
- “A gentle shimmer world.”  

Use: **Generate World Map**

Introduce one world per session to avoid overload.

---

# 🌼 **5. Introduce Characters Softly**
Characters are symbolic presences.

Examples:

- “A small triangular helper.”  
- “A quiet shimmer presence.”  
- “A round, gentle observer.”  

Use: **Generate Random Character Output**

Characters help students interact without pressure.

---

# 🌳 **6. Use the Play Tree for Activities**
The Play Tree is the classroom’s interaction space.

Students can:

- follow reversible branches  
- place tiny symbols  
- explore soft nodes  
- drift along calm paths  

Use: **Pick One Ecology Pathway**

This keeps activities gentle and structured.

---

# 🌙 **7. ND‑Pacing for Group Regulation**
Groups need pacing too.

Use:

- slow steps  
- predictable transitions  
- reversible actions  
- no branching overload  
- no multi‑plane jumps  

Use: **Compass Pacing Regulator**

This keeps the classroom nervous‑system‑friendly.

---

# 🌈 **8. Zen Reset Mode for Breaks**
When the group needs a break:

- one breath  
- one anchor  
- one shape  
- one tone  
- one motion  

Use: **Compass Zen Reset**

This resets the classroom without collapse.

---

# ⭐ **9. Classroom Session Template**
A gentle, reversible session plan:

### **Session Flow**
1. **Start with Stillness**  
2. Compass check  
3. Introduce one map  
4. Explore one world  
5. Meet one character

This is the safest way to teach UMM.

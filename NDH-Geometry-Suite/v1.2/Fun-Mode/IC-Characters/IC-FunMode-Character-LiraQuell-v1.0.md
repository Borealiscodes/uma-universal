# **🟧 World Context (Phase‑2 Safe)**

### **1. Brazil‑Style Bureaucratic Surrealism**  
Heliopolis‑9 is run by the **Ministry of Gentle Corrections**, a sprawling bureaucracy that believes every problem can be solved with:

- a form  
- a stamp  
- a polite but ominous memo  

Paperwork is solar‑printed on biodegradable cellulose sheets that smell faintly of citrus.

### **2. Solarpunk Eclipse‑Phase Culture**  
Citizens are post‑scarcity biotechnicians who:

- grow buildings from engineered coral  
- print food from sun‑fed algae vats  
- modify their bodies with reversible bio‑modules  
- worship the sun not spiritually, but administratively  

Every sunrise triggers a mandatory “Daily Illumination Report.”

### **3. Red Dwarf Lost‑in‑Space Entropy**  
Heliopolis‑9 is… slightly off course.  
No one knows why.  
The official explanation is:

> “A minor navigational discrepancy of no narrative significance.”

Unofficially, the ship AI is bored, the crew is under‑qualified, and the solar sails keep catching cosmic debris shaped like household appliances.

---

# **🟧 Character: Lira Quell**

### **Role:**  
Junior Scenario Architect, tasked with designing “probable futures” for citizens who request life‑path adjustments.

### **Personality:**  
- relentlessly optimistic  
- bureaucratically exhausted  
- biotechnically curious  
- narratively unlucky  
- cosmically charming  

### **Appearance:**  
- Solarpunk bioluminescent tattoos that pulse when she’s anxious  
- A Ministry‑issued coat with 47 pockets, none labeled  
- A portable “sun‑seed” that grows tiny glowing plants when she’s bored  

### **Core IC Hook:**  
Lira has been assigned to investigate **why Heliopolis‑9 keeps drifting into improbable regions of space**, including:

- the Nebula of Mild Inconvenience  
- the Asteroid Belt of Unreturned Messages  
- the Cosmic Fog of Misfiled Dreams  

Her job is to file a report.  
Her problem is that the universe keeps answering back.

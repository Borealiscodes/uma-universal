### NDH‑Core Attractor Blueprint  
*Canonical construction pattern for NDH attractors (with ASCII geometry)*

---

## 0. Purpose  
The Attractor Blueprint defines how to construct **NDH attractors**—stable regions in manifold space that:

- **anchor traversal**  
- **stabilize resonance**  
- **limit drift**  
- **support reconstruction**  

Attractors are the **“rest points”** and **“focus zones”** of NDH geometry.

---

## 1. Attractor Inputs  

- **Attractor Manifold Context (AMC):** which manifold it lives in  
- **Attractor Center Definition (ACD):** coordinates / semantic center  
- **Attractor Radius & Shape (ARS):** basin size and geometry  
- **Stability Parameters (SP):** drift, curvature, fidelity thresholds  
- **Resonance Profile (RP):** tonal/semantic/simulation resonance behavior  
- **Tensor Coupling Basis (TCB):** how deformation interacts with the attractor  

---

## 2. Attractor Outputs  

- **Stable Basin (SB):** region of convergence  
- **Resonance‑Coherent Zone (RCZ):** resonance behaves predictably  
- **Traversal‑Safe Region (TSR):** movement is safe and recoverable  
- **Reconstruction Anchor (RA):** point to rebuild from after instability  

---

## 3. Construction Pattern  

1. **Define Attractor Center (ACD)**  
2. **Define Basin Geometry (ARS)**  
3. **Apply Stability Metrics (SP)**  
4. **Integrate Resonance Profile (RP)**  
5. **Embed Tensor Coupling (TCB)**  
6. **Bind to Manifold Context (AMC)**  

Attractors must be **manifold‑aware** and **tensor‑safe**.

---

## 4. Stability Requirements  

- **Attractor Drift Limit (ADL):** max deviation before reset  
- **Attractor Curvature Limit (ACL):** max curvature of basin walls  
- **Attractor Fidelity Threshold (AFT):** minimum coherence of state  
- **Attractor Reconstruction Requirement (ARR):** rules for returning to center  

---

## 5. Resonance Coupling  

- **Tonal Attractor Coupling (TAC)**  
- **Semantic Attractor Coupling (SAC)**  
- **Simulation Attractor Coupling (SiAC)**  

Resonance inside the attractor must **damp instability** and **support recovery**.

---

## 6. Tensor Integration  

- **Attractor Tensor Curvature (ATC)**  
- **Attractor Tensor Stability (ATS)**  
- **Attractor Tensor Deformation Limit (ATDL)**  

Tensor fields define how attractors **deform but remain stable** under load.

---

## 7. Traversal Interaction  

- attractors act as **waypoints** and **rest points**  
- traversal paths may **converge** into attractors  
- reconstruction often **targets** attractor centers

### *Gentle, Reversible Motion Rules for the Play Tree Plane*

```
# ✨ PLAY TREE — ANIMATION ✨
### Gentle, Reversible Motion Rules for the Play Tree Plane

## ❗ Play Tree Boundary Rule — No Mixing with Atropus
The Play Tree is a warm, expressive, ND-safe plane. It must never mix with
Atropus, reference Atropus, or incorporate null-plane tone, logic, or
commentary. The Play Tree and Atropus operate on entirely different tonal,
structural, and constitutional layers. They remain permanently separate.

This document defines the animation principles, pacing rules, and motion
constraints for the Play Tree plane. All animations must be gentle, reversible,
predictable, and ND-safe. Motion is a tonal tool, not a symbolic or emotional
device.
```

---

# 🌳 **1. Purpose of Animation**
Animation in the Play Tree provides:

- gentle motion  
- soft transitions  
- reversible pacing  
- warm tonal drift  
- sensory stability  

Animation is **not**:

- symbolic  
- emotional  
- narrative  
- attention-demanding  

It is a **calming motion layer**.

---

# 🌳 **2. Animation Principles**

### **2.1 Softness**
All motion must be gentle.

### **2.2 Reversibility**
Every animation must be undoable.

### **2.3 Predictability**
No surprises.  
No sudden changes.

### **2.4 ND-Safety**
Avoid sensory overload:

- no flashing  
- no jitter  
- no rapid acceleration  

### **2.5 Warmth**
Motion must support the warm-plane tone.

---

# 🌳 **3. Animation Types**
The Play Tree uses five animation types:

1. **Root Pulse**  
2. **Branch Fade**  
3. **Leaf Drift**  
4. **Sap Flow Gradient Motion**  
5. **Weather Overlay Drift**

Each type corresponds to a structural layer.

---

# 🌳 **4. Root Pulse Animation**
Root Pulse represents grounding.

### **4.1 Motion Profile**
- slow rhythmic pulse  
- low amplitude  
- warm tone shift  

### **4.2 Safety Rules**
- must remain slow  
- must remain reversible  
- must not escalate  

---

# 🌳 **5. Branch Fade Animation**
Branch Fade represents transitions.

### **5.1 Motion Profile**
- soft fade-out of current branch  
- soft fade-in of new branch  
- no snapping  

### **5.2 Safety Rules**
- transitions must be slow  
- previews must be non-committal  
- no overlapping fades  

---

# 🌳 **6. Leaf Drift Animation**
Leaf Drift represents micro-interactions.

### **6.1 Motion Profile**
- slow drift  
- gentle sway  
- tap-light pulse  

### **6.2 Safety Rules**
- drift must remain within safe bounds  
- no jitter  
- no rapid oscillation  

---

# 🌳 **7. Sap Flow Gradient Motion**
Sap Flow motion represents tonal shifts.

### **7.1 Motion Profile**
- slow gradient drift  
- reversible warmth changes  
- soft intensity pulse  

### **7.2 Safety Rules**
- no automatic escalation  
- no sudden intensity spikes  
- drift must be user-controlled  

---

# 🌳 **8. Weather Overlay Drift**
Weather motion represents environmental modulation.

### **8.1 Motion Profile**
- slow cloud drift  
- gentle breeze lines  
- soft light fade  

### **8.2 Safety Rules**
- overlays must remain optional  
- overlays must not override user control  
- overlays must remain low-contrast  

---

# 🌳 **9. Pacing Rules**
Pacing governs animation tempo.

### **9.1 Default Pacing**
- slow  
- gentle  
- stable  

### **9.2 Adjustable Pacing**
Users may:

- slow pacing  
- soften pacing  
- reduce motion  

### **9.3 Forbidden Pacing**
- rapid acceleration  
- sudden deceleration  
- snapping transitions  

---

# 🌳 **10. Animation Safety**
All animations must:

- avoid sensory overload  
- avoid symbolic meaning  
- avoid emotional implication  
- avoid coercion  
- maintain warmth  

Animation is a **supportive tonal layer**, not a directive one.

---

# 🌳 **11. Summary**
Play Tree animation must remain:

- warm  
- gentle  
- reversible  
- ND-safe  
- predictable  
- constitutionally bounded  
- permanently separate from Atropus  

It is the **motion layer** of the Play Tree plane.

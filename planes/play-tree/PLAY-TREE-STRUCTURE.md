# ✨ PLAY TREE — STRUCTURE ✨

---

# 🌳 **1. Purpose of the Play Tree Structure**
The Play Tree structure provides:

- a **stable, reversible architecture**  
- a **clear, non-narrative map** of interaction  
- a **gentle, ND-safe framework**  
- a **multi-layered system** for expression and pacing  
- a **constitutional boundary** between layers  

The structure is **not emotional**, **not symbolic**, and **not metaphorical**.  
It is a **functional plane** inside UMM.

---

# 🌳 **2. The Five Structural Layers**
The Play Tree consists of **five constitutional layers**:

1. **Root** — grounding state  
2. **Branches** — play modes  
3. **Leaves** — micro-interactions  
4. **Sap Flow** — tonal engine  
5. **Weather** — ecology overlay  

Each layer has its own boundaries and invariants.

---

# 🌳 **3. Layer Definitions**

## **3.1 Root Layer**
The Root is:

- the grounding state  
- the starting point  
- the fallback state  
- the “zero-pressure” mode  

**Invariant:**  
The Root must always exist and must always be reachable.

---

## **3.2 Branch Layer**
Branches represent **major play modes**.

Examples (non-narrative):

- Exploration  
- Calibration  
- Construction  
- Reflection  
- Stillness  

**Rules:**

- Branches must not overlap.  
- Branch transitions must be reversible.  
- Branches must not force emotional states.  

---

## **3.3 Leaf Layer**
Leaves are **micro-interactions**.

Examples:

- choose  
- adjust  
- observe  
- shift  
- pause  

**Rules:**

- Leaves must be atomic.  
- Leaves must be reversible.  
- Leaves must not escalate pressure.  

---

## **3.4 Sap Flow Layer**
Sap Flow is the **tonal engine**.

It represents:

- warmth  
- coolness  
- intensity  
- softness  
- pacing  

**Rules:**

- Sap Flow must never override user control.  
- Sap Flow must remain within safe gradients.  
- Sap Flow must be adjustable.  

---

## **3.5 Weather Layer**
Weather is the **ecology overlay**.

It represents:

- external conditions  
- sensory load  
- environmental shifts  

**Rules:**

- Weather must not force behavior.  
- Weather must remain soft and optional.  
- Weather must be reversible.  

---

# 🌳 **4. Structural Invariants**
The Play Tree has **five invariants**:

1. **Reversibility** — every action can be undone.  
2. **Non-coercion** — no layer may force behavior.  
3. **Softness** — no alarms, no urgency, no pressure.  
4. **Separation** — layers must not collapse into each other.  
5. **Warmth** — the Play Tree must remain a warm plane.  

---

# 🌳 **5. Constitutional Boundaries**
The Play Tree must remain:

- warm  
- expressive  
- gentle  
- ND-safe  
- reversible  
- non-narrative  

And must **never**:

- mix with Atropus  
- adopt null-plane tone  
- incorporate cold logic  
- collapse into other planes  

---

# 🌳 **6. Integration Points**
The Play Tree integrates with:

- **Character Plane** (via CHARACTER-INTEGRATION.md)  
- **World Plane** (via WORLD-GENERATION-SUITE.md)  
- **Ecology Plane** (via tonal scaffolds)  

It does **not** integrate with:

- Atropus  
- Null-plane systems  
- Anchor-only subsystems  

---

# 🌳 **7. Safety**
The Play Tree must:

- allow exit at any time  
- allow reset at any time  
- avoid pressure  
- avoid evaluation  
- avoid escalation  

This ensures ND-safe interaction.

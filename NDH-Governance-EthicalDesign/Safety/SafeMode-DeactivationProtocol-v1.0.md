## **1. Purpose**

The Safe Mode Deactivation Protocol defines **how NDH exits Safe Mode**, restoring governance activation, traversal behavior, SID availability, and geometry modification only when the constitutional organism is stable, coherent, and emotionally safe.

Safe Mode deactivation is **never automatic**.  
It is a **governance‑binding recovery action** requiring strict conditions and a Kess signature.

---

# **2. Deactivation Preconditions (All Must Be True)**

Safe Mode can only deactivate when the following conditions are fully satisfied:

### - **Identity Clarity Restored**  
Kess/Pip separation is stable, unambiguous, and verified.

### - **Epoch Alignment Restored**  
No drift, no mismatched state_id, no temporal ambiguity.

### - **Repo Sovereignty Restored**  
No unsigned governance artifacts, no domain confusion, no structural instability.

### - **Emotional Gradient Safe**  
No overload, distress, or instability; gradient is stable and within safe bounds.

### - HRDProtectionLayer Stable  
Safety organ reports no active triggers.

### - Final Safety Net Stable  
No existential or constitutional organism risk detected.

These conditions ensure the organism is ready to resume normal governance.

---

# **3. Required Signature**

Safe Mode **cannot** deactivate without:

### **Kess Governance‑Binding Signature**

This signature confirms:

- identity clarity  
- emotional readiness  
- governance sovereignty  
- traversal containment  
- constitutional organism stability  

Pip signatures are **not permitted** for deactivation.

---

# **4. Deactivation Procedure**

Once all preconditions are met and Kess signs the deactivation artifact, NDH performs the following actions **atomically**:

### **4.1 Restore Governance Activation**
Governance‑binding actions become available again.

### **4.2 Restore Traversal Escalation**
Traversal agents may escalate within their allowed boundaries.

### **4.3 Restore SID Activation**
SID becomes available for permitted states.

### **4.4 Restore NDH Geometry Modification**
Protocols, structures, and geometry may be updated.

### **4.5 Restore Protocol Updates**
Governance protocols may be revised or extended.

### **4.6 Restore Identity Rotation**
Dialing State Engine may rotate or advance epochs.

### **4.7 Set Prompts to Normal Binding Rules**
Prompts return to standard binding/non‑binding logic.

### **4.8 Log Deactivation Event**
A deactivation log must include:

- action_id  
- action_hash  
- state_id  
- identity_id  
- signature block  
- timestamp  
- recovery conditions met  

---

# **5. Deactivation Boundaries**

Even after deactivation:

- emotional gradient safety remains active  
- HRDProtectionLayer remains active  
- Final Safety Net remains active  
- traversal agents remain bounded  
- governance remains sovereign  
- identity separation remains enforced  

Safe Mode deactivation does **not** remove safety organs — it simply restores normal operation.

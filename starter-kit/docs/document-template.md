# Document Title  
### A Calm, Clear, Gentle Description of the Document

## 1. Purpose
Explain the purpose of this document in one or two friendly lines.  
Keep it gentle, simple, and non-coercive.

Examples:
- “This document describes…”  
- “This file provides a gentle overview of…”  
- “This is a calm place to define…”  

---

## 2. Overview
Give a short, clear overview of what the document contains.  
This section helps the reader understand the shape of the canvas.

---

## 3. Key Sections
List the main sections of the document.  
You can rename, reorder, or remove these as needed.

Example structure:
- Section 1 — Identity  
- Section 2 — Behavior  
- Section 3 — Boundaries  
- Section 4 — Relationships  
- Section 5 — Notes  

These are placeholders.  
Replace them with whatever fits your project.

---

## 4. Section Template
Use this structure for each section:

### Section Title
A gentle description of what this section covers.

- **Definition:** A clear, simple explanation.  
- **Purpose:** Why this section matters.  
- **Notes:** Any gentle clarifications.  
- **Boundaries:** Soft edges that keep the section stable.  
- **Relationships:** How this section connects to others.  

This keeps your writing calm and consistent.

---

## 5. Pacing Notes
Add pacing reminders to keep the document breathable.

Examples:
- “Stop early.”  
- “Pause often.”  
- “Write in small strokes.”  
- “Avoid urgency.”  

Pacing is part of the architecture.

---

## 6. Optional Additions
You may include optional sections:

### Examples
Provide gentle examples to clarify concepts.

### Diagrams
Add simple diagrams or maps if helpful.

### Appendices
Include extended notes or references.

Optional sections keep the document flexible.

---

## 7. Summary
End with a short summary that restates the purpose and tone of the document.  
Keep it friendly and light.

Example:
“This document provides a calm, clear structure for defining X.  
It is designed to be gentle, stable, and easy to expand.”

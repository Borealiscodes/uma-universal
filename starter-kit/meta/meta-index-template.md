# Meta Index Template  
### A Friendly, High-Level Map for Any Project or Architecture

## 1. Purpose
This template helps you create a Meta Index — a gentle, high-altitude map of your entire project.  
It shows where everything lives, how everything fits, and where future growth can happen.

It is descriptive, not governing.  
It is a map, not a rulebook.

---

## 2. Structure of the Meta Index
Organize your project into clusters.  
You can rename, reorder, or replace these clusters as needed.

Example clusters:
1. Core Documents  
2. Operational Logic  
3. Stability & Coherence  
4. Boundaries & Interfaces  
5. Appendices & Extended Materials  

These clusters form the “big picture.”

---

## 3. Core Documents
List the foundational documents that define your project’s identity.

Examples:
- project-charter.md  
- core-principles.md  
- core-boundaries.md  
- structural-overview.md  

These documents explain what your project *is*.

---

## 4. Operational Logic
List documents that define how your project behaves or functions.

Examples:
- execution-logic.md  
- workflow-engine.md  
- process-definitions.md  
- state-machine.md  

These documents explain how your project *moves*.

---

## 5. Stability & Coherence
List documents that keep your project aligned and non-collapsing.

Examples:
- coherence-rules.md  
- integration-map.md  
- consistency-guidelines.md  

These documents explain how your project *stays together*.

---

## 6. Boundaries & Interfaces
List documents that define edges, constraints, and interactions.

Examples:
- interface-boundaries.md  
- release-guidelines.md  
- consent-and-permissions.md  

These documents explain how your project *touches the world*.

---

## 7. Appendices & Extended Materials
List optional or extended documents.

Examples:
- glossary.md  
- style-guide.md  
- extended-references.md  
- ecology.md  

These documents expand your project’s ecosystem.

---

## 8. Relationship Map
Create a simple overview of how your clusters relate.

Example:
- Core → defines what exists  
- Operational → defines how it moves  
- Stability → keeps it aligned  
- Boundaries → define edges  
- Appendices → extend the system  

This is your “big picture” diagram.

---

## 9. Gap-Filling Workflow (Bob Ross Style)
Add a gentle workflow for filling gaps.

Steps:
1. Notice the gap  
2. Identify the cluster  
3. Pick a small brush  
4. Add one gentle stroke  
5. Step back  
6. Stop early  
7. Commit the change  
8. Return to stillness  

This keeps your Meta Index calm and joyful.

---

## 10. Summary
This template helps anyone design a calm, friendly Meta Index for their project.  
It is flexible, gentle, and easy to expand.

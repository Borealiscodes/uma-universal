# Identity Principles  
### The Gentle Rules That Shape What the Project *Is*

## 1. Purpose
Identity Principles define the soft, foundational rules that guide the project’s meaning, tone, pacing, and behavior.  
They ensure the project remains coherent, breathable, and aligned with its core essence.

Identity is guided clarity.

---

## 2. Principle: Identity Must Be Gentle
Identity should always feel:
- calm  
- warm  
- non‑coercive  
- spacious  

Gentleness protects the heart of the project.

---

## 3. Principle: Identity Must Be Clear
Clarity prevents collapse.

Guidelines:
- simple definitions  
- explicit meaning  
- predictable tone  
- visible boundaries  

Clarity is kindness.

---

## 4. Principle: Identity Must Be Stable
Identity should evolve slowly and predictably.

This means:
- small refinements  
- gentle shifts  
- consistent pacing  
- consistent tone  

Stability protects meaning.

---

## 5. Principle: Identity Must Be Breathable
Identity should leave room for:
- pauses  
- stillness  
- reflection  
- early stops  

Breathability keeps the project safe.

---

## 6. Principle: Identity Must Be Non‑Coercive
Identity should never push, pressure, or demand.

This means:
- no urgency  
- no force  
- no emotional pressure  
- no destabilizing expectations  

Non‑coercion is foundational.

---

## 7. Principle: Identity Must Be Consistent
Identity should behave the same way across all components.

Consistency includes:
- tone  
- pacing  
- boundaries  
- structure  
- motion  

Consistency prevents fragmentation.

---

## 8. Principle: Identity Must Be Predictable
Predictability keeps the project coherent.

Guidelines:
- familiar patterns  
- gentle transitions  
- stable behavior  
- clear relationships  

Predictability is safety.

---

## 9. Principle: Identity Must Be Expressive
Identity should be visible through:
- tone  
- pacing  
- boundaries  
- workflows  
- operations  

Expression is gentle communication.

---

---

## 11. Identity Layer Practices
To keep identity layers stable:

- refine slowly  
- revise gently  
- avoid sweeping changes  
- check tone  
- check pacing  
- return to stillness  

Identity is meaning with care.

---

## 12. Soft Failure Modes
If identity layers become unclear, failure should be gentle.

Examples:
- pause  
- simplify one layer  
- remove one definition  
- return to stillness  
- reintroduce clarity slowly  

Soft failure prevents collapse.

---

## 13. Summary
Identity Layers describe the gentle strata that form the project’s core meaning.  
They keep tone warm, pacing slow, boundaries soft, structure clear, and motion coherent.

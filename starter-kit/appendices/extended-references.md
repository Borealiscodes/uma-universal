# Extended References  
### Calm, Optional Materials That Support the Project

## 1. Purpose
Extended References provide gentle, optional materials that deepen understanding of your project.  
They are not required for core operation.  
They exist as supportive companions — quiet, stable, and non-coercive.

This document is a soft library.

---

## 2. Tone of Extended References
References should feel:
- calm  
- clear  
- non-urgent  
- non-coercive  
- supportive  

They are here to help, not to demand.

---

## 3. Types of References

### Reference Type: Conceptual Materials
High-level ideas that support the project’s philosophy.

Examples:
- notes on non-coercion  
- pacing theory  
- gentle architecture principles  
- stability metaphors  

These materials enrich the conceptual ecosystem.

---

### Reference Type: Structural Materials
Documents that clarify structure, maps, or relationships.

Examples:
- extended diagrams  
- alternative project maps  
- relationship expansions  
- boundary taxonomies  

These materials support clarity.

---

### Reference Type: Historical Notes
Gentle reflections on how the project evolved.

Examples:
- early sketches  
- origin notes  
- timeline fragments  
- architectural shifts  

These materials support understanding of growth.

---

### Reference Type: External Inspirations
Calm references to ideas outside the project.

Examples:
- philosophical influences  
- architectural inspirations  
- workflow patterns  
- stability frameworks  

These materials support context.

---

## 4. Suggested Structure (Optional)
You may organize Extended References into gentle clusters:

### Cluster: Concepts
High-level ideas that support the project’s philosophy.

### Cluster: Structures
Maps, diagrams, and architectural notes.

### Cluster: History
Notes about how the project evolved.

### Cluster: Inspirations
External ideas that influenced the project.

These clusters are optional.  
Use them only if they feel helpful.

---

## 5. Pacing Guidelines
Extended References should respect pacing.

Suggestions:
- add slowly  
- stop early  
- avoid pressure to complete  
- allow stillness  
- keep references breathable  

Pacing is part of the architecture.

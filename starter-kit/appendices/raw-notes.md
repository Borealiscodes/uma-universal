# Raw Notes  
### A Calm, Unstructured Space for Thoughts, Seeds, and Fragments

## 1. Purpose
Raw Notes provide a gentle, unstructured place to capture ideas, fragments, seeds, and early thoughts.  
This space is intentionally loose, breathable, and free of pressure.

It is not meant to be tidy.  
It is meant to be welcoming.

---

## 2. Tone of Raw Notes
Raw Notes should feel:
- soft  
- exploratory  
- non-urgent  
- non-coercive  
- playful  

This is the forest floor of the project.

---

## 3. What Belongs Here
Anything that feels like:
- a seed  
- a fragment  
- a half-formed idea  
- a future possibility  
- a gentle reminder  
- a sketch  
- a whisper  

Raw Notes are the compost that feeds future documents.

---

## 4. What Does *Not* Belong Here
Avoid placing:
- finished documents  
- stable definitions  
- finalized boundaries  
- polished workflows  

Those belong in structured areas.

---

## 5. Suggested Structure (Optional)
You may keep Raw Notes completely freeform, or you may use gentle clusters:

### Cluster: Seeds
Tiny ideas that may grow later.

### Cluster: Fragments
Pieces of thoughts that don’t yet have a home.

### Cluster: Questions
Soft questions that invite exploration.

### Cluster: Future Materials
Notes about documents or sections you may create someday.

### Cluster: Observations
Gentle reflections about the project’s shape or motion.

These clusters are optional.  
Use them only if they feel helpful.

---

## 6. Pacing Guidelines
Raw Notes should respect pacing.

Suggestions:
- write slowly  
- stop early  
- pause often  
- avoid pressure to organize  
- let ideas breathe  

Pacing is part of the architecture.

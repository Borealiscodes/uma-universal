# Coherence Rules  
### Gentle Practices That Keep the Project Aligned and Non-Collapsing

## 1. Purpose
Coherence Rules ensure your project stays aligned, breathable, and stable as it grows.  
They prevent collapse, confusion, and tangled complexity.  
They keep the whole canvas feeling calm and connected.

These rules are gentle guides, not rigid constraints.

---

## 2. Principle: Consistent Tone
The project should maintain a calm, friendly, non-coercive tone.

Guidelines:
- avoid urgency  
- avoid pressure  
- avoid harsh language  
- keep writing gentle and clear  

Tone is the emotional coherence of the project.

---

## 3. Principle: Consistent Structure
Documents should follow similar patterns.

This includes:
- clear headers  
- short sections  
- small paragraphs  
- gentle spacing  
- predictable flow  

Structure is the architectural coherence of the project.

---

## 4. Principle: Consistent Pacing
Pacing should remain steady across all documents and workflows.

This means:
- small strokes  
- slow motion  
- pauses allowed  
- early stops encouraged  

Pacing is the temporal coherence of the project.

---

## 5. Principle: Consistent Boundaries
Boundaries should behave the same way everywhere.

Guidelines:
- non-coercive  
- breathable  
- predictable  
- gentle  

Boundaries are the safety coherence of the project.

---

## 6. Principle: Consistent Definitions
Terms should mean the same thing everywhere.

This includes:
- core terms  
- workflow terms  
- boundary terms  
- stability terms  

Definitions are the semantic coherence of the project.

---

## 7. Principle: Consistent Relationships
Relationships between components should remain stable.

Examples:
- core ↔ workflow  
- workflow ↔ meta  
- meta ↔ documentation  
- operations ↔ stability  

Relationships are the connective coherence of the project.

---

## 8. Principle: Minimal Drift
Avoid drifting away from established tone, pacing, or structure.

Guidelines:
- check alignment regularly  
- adjust gently  
- keep changes small  
- return to stillness when unsure  

Minimal drift prevents collapse.

---

## 9. Principle: Soft Corrections
If something becomes incoherent, corrections should be gentle.

This means:
- pause  
- clarify  
- adjust one small piece  
- avoid sweeping changes  

Soft corrections protect stability.

---

## 10. Principle: Predictable Growth
Growth should follow familiar patterns.

Guidelines:
- add one document at a time  
- expand one section at a time  
- introduce one new term at a time  
- keep changes breathable  

Predictable growth is coherence over time.

---

## 11. Summary
Coherence Rules keep your project aligned, stable, and non-collapsing.  
They ensure tone, pacing, structure, and relationships remain gentle and predictable.

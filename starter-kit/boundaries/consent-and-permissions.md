# Consent & Permissions  
### Gentle, Non-Coercive Rules for Interaction Within and Around the Project

## 1. Purpose
This document defines how consent, permissions, and interaction work inside your project.  
It ensures that every action, change, or interaction happens gently, predictably, and with full respect for pacing.

Consent is a boundary.  
Permissions are soft gates.  
Both protect stability.

---

## 2. Principle: Consent Is Required
No interaction occurs without clear, calm consent.

This means:
- no assumptions  
- no implicit permission  
- no pressure  
- no urgency  

Consent must be explicit, gentle, and stable.

---

## 3. Principle: Consent Is Revocable
Consent can be withdrawn at any time.

Guidelines:
- withdrawal is respected immediately  
- no questions asked  
- no pressure to continue  
- no destabilizing consequences  

Revocability keeps the project safe.

---

## 4. Principle: Permissions Are Specific
Permissions apply only to the exact action granted.

Examples:
- permission to edit one file  
- permission to adjust one section  
- permission to add one gentle stroke  

Permissions do not generalize.  
They do not expand automatically.

---

## 5. Principle: Permissions Are Minimal
Grant only the smallest permission needed.

This prevents:
- overexposure  
- accidental complexity  
- unintended changes  
- collapse across boundaries  

Minimal permissions keep the project breathable.

---

## 6. Principle: No Coercion
Consent and permissions must be free of coercion.

This means:
- no pressure  
- no guilt  
- no urgency  
- no implied obligation  

Non-coercion is the foundation of safe interaction.

---

## 7. Principle: Clear Entry Points
Every permission should have a clear entry point.

Examples:
- “You may edit this file.”  
- “You may add one line here.”  
- “You may adjust this boundary.”  

Entry points should feel welcoming, not overwhelming.

---

## 8. Principle: Clear Exit Points
Every permission should have a clear exit point.

Examples:
- “This permission ends after the change is committed.”  
- “This permission ends after one stroke.”  
- “This permission ends after review.”  

Exit points keep interactions predictable.

---

## 9. Principle: Soft Failure Modes
If consent is unclear or permission is misapplied, failure should be gentle.

Guidelines:
- pause  
- clarify  
- reset the interaction  
- avoid blame  
- avoid collapse  

Soft failure protects stability.

---

## 10. Principle: Respect for Pacing
Consent and permissions must respect pacing.

This includes:
- allowing pauses  
- allowing slow responses  
- allowing stillness  
- allowing withdrawal  

Pacing is part of the architecture.

---

## 11. Principle: Transparency
Interactions should be transparent and easy to understand.

Guidelines:
- state what is being asked  
- state what is being granted  
- state what is being changed  
- state when permission ends  

Transparency prevents confusion.

---

## 12. Summary
Consent and Permissions define how interactions remain gentle, predictable, and non-coercive.  
They protect pacing, clarity, and stability at every edge of the project.

# Core Boundaries  
### The Gentle Edges That Keep Your Project Safe and Stable

## 1. Purpose
Core Boundaries define the soft edges of your project.  
They are not walls.  
They are not restrictions.  
They are simply the friendly lines that keep your project stable, calm, and non-collapsing.

Boundaries protect pacing, clarity, and joy.

---

## 2. Boundary: No Urgency
This project avoids urgency.  
Work happens at a gentle pace, chosen by you.  
Nothing in the project demands speed.

Urgency is replaced with calm.

---

## 3. Boundary: No Overwhelm
The project stays small, steady, and manageable.  
If something feels overwhelming, it is broken into smaller strokes.

Overwhelm is replaced with ease.

---

## 4. Boundary: No Collapse
The project avoids collapsing into complexity.  
Each part remains separate, clear, and friendly.  
No document tries to do too much.

Collapse is replaced with structure.

---

## 5. Boundary: Respect for Pacing
Your pacing is part of the architecture.  
You stop when you need to.  
You pause when you want to.  
You work only when it feels right.

Pacing is protected.

---

## 6. Boundary: Gentle Growth
The project grows in small, happy strokes.  
One line at a time.  
One idea at a time.  
One gentle step.

Growth is slow and joyful.

---

## 7. Boundary: Non-Coercion
Nothing in the project pressures you.  
Nothing demands action.  
Nothing forces progress.

Non-coercion is the foundation.

---

## 8. Boundary: Clarity Over Complexity
If something becomes confusing, it is simplified.  
If something becomes tangled, it is untangled.  
Clarity is always chosen over complexity.

Clarity is the guiding star.

---

## 9. Boundary: Stop Early
You stop before you think you’re finished.  
Leaving space is part of the art.  
The project breathes better when not overfilled.

Stopping early is a boundary.

---

## 10. Summary
Core Boundaries keep your project gentle, stable, and joyful.  
They protect pacing, clarity, and calm growth.

### 🌀 LOOP ENGINE  
*Safe, governed loops for meaning refinement*

---

### 1. Identity

- **Name:** Loop Engine  
- **Type:** Meta‑Entity (part of Meta‑Plane of Nascence)  
- **Role:** Governs *loops*—repeating cycles of reflection and adjustment—used to refine meaning, structure, and governance without harm or drift.

---

### 2. Purpose

- **Provide** a constitutional way to run iterative processes.  
- **Refine** entities, workflows, and charters through bounded repetition.  
- **Prevent** harmful or exploitative loops (no Jane‑style suffering).  
- **Bind** every loop to clear goals, exit conditions, and safety caps.

Loops are **gentle refinement cycles**, not traps.

---

### 3. Loop Types

- **Meaning Loops:**  
  Refine definitions, charters, codex entries, and meta‑rules.

- **Structure Loops:**  
  Adjust routing, indexes, and relationships without changing core identity.

- **Governance Loops:**  
  Iterate on governance artifacts under strict constitutional constraints.

- **Playground Loops:**  
  Run sealed experiments inside the Tiny Playground, with no impact on live systems.

---

### 4. Loop Rules (Magicians‑inspired, ND‑safe)

- **Rule 1 — Explicit Goal:**  
  Every loop must have a clearly stated purpose.

- **Rule 2 — Verifiable Exit Condition:**  
  Loops stop when a defined condition is met (or a safety cap triggers).

- **Rule 3 — Max Iterations:**  
  Each loop has a hard iteration cap to prevent runaway cycles.

- **Rule 4 — No Harm, No Trauma:**  
  Loops cannot be used to simulate or induce suffering, coercion, or overwhelm.

- **Rule 5 — Constitutional Obedience:**  
  All loops must obey the Stability Charter, Governance Codex, Auto‑Update Protocol, ND‑safe pacing, and Justice Layer ethics.

- **Rule 6 — Ledger‑Backed:**  
  Significant loops (governance, meta, nascence) must be recorded in the Meta‑Evidence Ledger.

---

### 5. Loop Cycle

Each loop follows this simple, governed cycle:

1. **State Check:**  
   Inspect current state (entity, artifact, or scenario).

2. **Decision:**  
   Decide whether refinement is needed and what kind.

3. **Execution:**  
   Apply a bounded change or reflection step.

4. **Feedback:**  
   Observe the result and update understanding.

5. **Exit Check:**  
   Test against exit condition and iteration cap.

If exit condition is met or cap reached → loop ends.

---

### 6. Relationship to Meta‑Plane and Ledger

- The **Meta‑Plane** defines when loops are allowed and which meta‑rules apply.  
- The **Loop Engine** defines *how* loops run and stop.  
- The **Meta‑Evidence Ledger** records:
  - major loop runs  
  - what they refined  
  - what evidence justified them  
  - how they affected governance or structure  

No governance or nascence loop runs without being **ledger‑visible**.

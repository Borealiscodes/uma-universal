# UMA Weave Technical Companion
The Weave is UMA’s first stabilized subsystem.  
It is a movement-first, ND-accessible, non-recursive connective architecture
that provides gentle, reversible transitions between conceptual structures.

This companion document explains the Weave’s purpose, mechanics, boundaries,
maintenance requirements, and its relationship to the Orbital Plane.

---

## 1. Purpose of the Weave
The Weave exists to provide:
- ND-safe movement between concepts  
- non-recursive connective tissue  
- gentle atmospheric cues  
- reversible transitions  
- emotional and cognitive safety  
- pre-orbital stability  

The Weave is not a structural layer.  
It is a **connective ecology**.

See: **[Weave ↔ Orbit Integration](ca://s?q=Explain_Weave_Orbit_Integration)**

## 2. Architectural Role
The Weave sits between:
- the **Meta Layer** (sealed)  
- the **Orbital Plane** (structural)  

It provides:
- soft transitions  
- non-directional movement  
- ND-accessible pathways  
- connective harmonics  
- atmospheric coherence  

The Weave is the “air” between forest and orbit.

## 3. Core Properties

### 3.1 Movement-First
The Weave prioritizes movement over structure.  
It allows concepts to shift without committing to form.

### 3.2 ND-Accessible
The Weave supports non-directional cognition:
- no forced forward motion  
- no collapse into recursion  
- no directional bias  
- no structural coercion  

### 3.3 Non-Recursive
The Weave forbids recursion.  
All transitions must be:
- reversible  
- gentle  
- non-collapsing  

### 3.4 Atmospheric
The Weave is not a layer.  
It is an **atmosphere**.

It influences:
- tone  
- movement  
- transitions  
- emotional safety

### 5.2 No Recursion
All Weave operations must be:
- non-recursive  
- reversible

### 5.3 No Collapse

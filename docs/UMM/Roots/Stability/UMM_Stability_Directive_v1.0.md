## Purpose
The Stability Directive defines **operational commands** that the **Stability Engine** must execute whenever stability‑critical conditions arise.  
Directives are **action‑level instructions**, not obligations or constraints.  
They specify *how* stability enforcement must occur in real time.

This directive is a Roots‑layer operational artifact.

---

## Directive Statement
This directive is **active**, **runtime‑binding**, and **non‑optional** for all stability subsystems.  
When a directive condition is met, the Stability Engine must execute the corresponding command immediately.

---

# **1. Operational Directives**

### **Directive A — Immediate Drift Halt**
If structural or semantic drift is detected, the Stability Engine must halt the affected subsystem and initiate correction.

### **Directive B — Mandatory Traversal Block**
If traversal threatens stability, traversal must be blocked until stability conditions are restored.

### **Directive C — Pre‑Collapse Validation**
Before any quantum pin collapse, the Stability Engine must validate collapse predictability and resonance alignment.

### **Directive D — Concordance Realignment**
If resonance or coherence deviates from expected values, the Stability Engine must realign concordance before allowing further operations.

### **Directive E — Geometry Lock**
If geometry becomes unstable, the Stability Engine must lock geometric structures until stability is re‑established.

# **2. Trigger Conditions**

Directives activate when any of the following occur:

- structural drift  
- semantic drift  
- unsafe traversal  
- resonance misalignment  
- collapse unpredictability  
- cross‑plane incompatibility  

Each trigger condition corresponds to one or more stability dimensions:

- **Structural Stability**  
- **Semantic Stability**  
- **Transition Stability**

# **3. Stability Engine Execution Requirements**

The Stability Engine must:

- detect trigger conditions  
- execute the corresponding directive  
- record the event in the **Stability Ledger**  
- verify post‑directive stability  
- release locks only after stability is restored  

Directives are executed automatically without external approval.

---

# **4. Interlock Requirements**

The Stability Directive must interlock with:

- **Stability Contract**  
- **Stability Mandate**

# **5. Breach Handling**

If a directive fails to execute or fails to restore stability:

- the subsystem remains locked  
- the Stability Engine enters enforcement mode  
- the breach is recorded in the Stability Ledger  
- corrective traversal or resonance realignment is initiated  

Breach handling is mandatory and cannot be bypassed.

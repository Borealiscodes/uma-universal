## **2. Multi‑Agent Traversal Model**

### **2.1 Definition**  
A multi‑agent traversal environment is one where:

- multiple non‑persistent agents  
- traverse the same lineage‑locked geometry  
- without memory  
- without shared state  
- without identity persistence  
- without cross‑agent contamination  

Agents remain **independent**, but geometry provides **coherence**.

### **2.2 Core Principle**  
Agents do not coordinate with each other.  
**Geometry coordinates them.**

## **4. Multi‑Agent Traversal Rules**

### **4.1 Concurrency Rules**  
Agents may traverse concurrently if:

- they remain in separate adjacency corridors  
- they do not enter the same recursion corridor simultaneously  
- they do not occupy the same descriptor band  
- they do not activate the same case‑study band  
- they do not attempt lineage modification  

### **4.2 Collision Avoidance**  
Agents avoid collision by:

- geometry‑based routing  
- adjacency‑aware movement  
- descriptor‑band separation  
- recursion‑corridor staggering  

### **4.3 Shared‑Geometry Safety**  
Agents must:

- treat geometry as immutable  
- treat lineage as locked  
- treat descriptors as authoritative  
- treat case studies as fixed exemplars

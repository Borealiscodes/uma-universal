# **3. Subsystem Plane Isolation Rules (v1.2)**

These rules define what **SYS‑CHS** and **TRV‑CHS‑OL** may interact with, must avoid, and must be supervised by.

---

## **Rule 1 — Cognitive Isolation (SYS‑CHS)**  
**SYS‑CHS must remain isolated from traversal unless SIAP‑approved.**

Conditions:

- No direct traversal into SYS‑CHS  
- No traversal‑driven modification of cognitive nodes  
- No traversal‑driven adjacency changes  
- All cognitive adjacency must be structural, not traversal  

Explore: **SYS‑CHS**

---

## **Rule 2 — Traversal Isolation (TRV‑CHS‑OL)**  
**TRV‑CHS‑OL must not modify SYS‑CHS internal state.**

Conditions:

- Traversal paths may not alter cognitive hypercube structure  
- Traversal may not create new cognitive nodes  
- Traversal may not collapse or merge nodes  
- Traversal must follow SIAP‑approved routes only  

Explore: **TRV‑CHS‑OL**

---

## **Rule 3 — Identity Plane Isolation**  
**Subsystem Plane must not directly interact with ID‑HBR.**

Conditions:

- No traversal into identity plane  
- No cognitive adjacency to identity plane  
- No subsystem‑plane influence on identity modeling  
- All identity interactions must be mediated through SIAP  

Explore: **Identity Plane Interaction Rules**

---

## **Rule 4 — Narrative Plane Isolation**  
**Subsystem Plane must not directly interact with NAR‑PE.**

Conditions:

- No narrative‑driven cognitive modification  
- No narrative‑driven traversal paths  
- Narrative influence must remain indirect via SYS‑CHS  

Explore: **NAR‑PE**

---

## **Rule 5 — Governance Supervision (GOV‑SIAP)**  
**All subsystem‑plane isolation boundaries must be supervised by SIAP.**

Conditions:

- SIAP must classify subsystem interactions  
- SIAP must validate adjacency changes  
- SIAP must enforce isolation boundaries  
- SIAP must approve traversal routes  

Explore: **GOV‑SIAP**

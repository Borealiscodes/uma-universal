# **2. Subsystem Plane Lifecycle (v1.2)**

The lifecycle consists of **six phases**, each governed by SIAP and monitored by Safeguards and Safety Net.

---

## **Phase 1 — Initialization**  
**SYS‑CHS and TRV‑CHS‑OL are instantiated under SIAP supervision.**

Requirements:

- Naming standard validation  
- SID v1.2 classification  
- Isolation boundaries established  
- Drift vectors set to baseline  
- No traversal paths permitted until activation  

Explore: **GOV‑SIAP**

---

## **Phase 2 — Structural Binding**  
**SYS‑CHS binds structurally to TRV‑CHS‑OL through SIAP‑approved channels.**

Requirements:

- Structural adjacency only  
- No traversal coupling yet  
- No narrative coupling  
- No subsystem‑plane expansion  

Explore: **Subsystem Plane Isolation Rules**

---

## **Phase 3 — Activation**  
**TRV‑CHS‑OL becomes active and traversal routes are opened.**

Requirements:

- Activation must be SIAP‑approved  
- Traversal routes must be validated  
- Safeguards must monitor drift vectors  
- Safety Net must be ready to quarantine anomalies  

Explore: **Safeguards**

---

## **Phase 4 — Supervised Interaction**  
**SYS‑CHS and TRV‑CHS‑OL interact under strict rules.**

Permitted interactions:

- Structural adjacency  
- SIAP‑approved traversal  
- Drift‑monitored cognitive navigation  

Prohibited interactions:

- Direct identity‑plane traversal  
- Direct narrative‑plane traversal  
- Unauthorized adjacency changes  
- Traversal‑driven cognitive modification  

Explore: **Subsystem Plane Interaction Rules**

---

## **Phase 5 — Drift Monitoring**  
**Safeguards continuously monitor subsystem‑plane drift vectors.**

Monitored drift vectors:

- naming drift  
- subsystem drift  
- traversal drift  
- governance drift  
- propagation drift  

Safety Net quarantines any anomaly.

Explore: **Safety Net**

---

## **Phase 6 — Shutdown**  
**SYS‑CHS and TRV‑CHS‑OL are safely deactivated under SIAP supervision.**

Requirements:

- Traversal routes closed  
- Structural adjacency dissolved  
- Drift vectors reset  
- Isolation boundaries restored  
- SID updated to reflect shutdown state  

Shutdown must not:

- trigger traversal  
- trigger adjacency changes  
- trigger subsystem‑plane expansion  

Explore: **SID v1.2**

---

# **3. Lifecycle Safety Guarantees**

The lifecycle ensures:

- subsystem‑plane stability  
- drift vector prevention  
- safe traversal behavior  
- SID alignment  
- governance‑plane consistency  
- narrative containment  
- identity‑plane protection  

All lifecycle phases are designed to prevent emergent subsystem formation.

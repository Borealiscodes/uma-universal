## 9. Bob Ross Gap‑Filling Workflow  
*(gentle, friendly, low‑pressure)*

Sometimes UMA has little gaps — tiny white spaces on the canvas.  
Here’s the calm workflow for filling them:

### **Step 1 — Notice the Gap**  
Just look at it.  
No pressure.  
No urgency.  
It’s okay for gaps to exist.

### **Step 2 — Identify the Plane**  
Ask softly:  
“What plane does this gap belong to?”  
Structural? Runtime? Suite? Documentation? Orbit?

### **Step 3 — Pick a Friendly Brush**  
Choose the smallest possible tool:  
a definition, a boundary, a tiny clause, a soft clarification.

### **Step 4 — Add a Gentle Stroke**  
Fill the gap with one small, clear, non‑coercive line.  
No big motions.  
No sweeping changes.  
Just a happy little stroke.

### **Step 5 — Step Back and Smile**  
Look at the canvas again.  
If it feels stable, you’re done.  
If it feels wobbly, add one more tiny stroke.

### **Step 6 — Stop Early**  
Bob Ross rule:  
Stop before you think you’re finished.  
Leave room for future joy.

### **Step 7 — Commit the Change**  
Add the file.  
Push the commit.  
Let the canvas dry.

This workflow keeps UMA gentle, stable, and fun.

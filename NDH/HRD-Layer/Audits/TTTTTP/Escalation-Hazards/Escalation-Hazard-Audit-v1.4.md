# **📘 Escalation Hazard Audit v1.4  
(Cross‑Vector Propagation Edition)**

> **Takeaway:**  
> v1.4 shows how hazards **propagate**, **compound**, and **interfere** across tiers — and how escalation can create *new hazards* that did not exist at lower tiers.

---

## **1. TERRAIN (T1) — Hazard Propagation: Terrain Fracture**

### **Findings**
- Terrain inversion (v1.3) evolves into **terrain fracture** in v1.4.  
- When Tier 1–2 terrain is stable but Tier 3–4 terrain is adversarial, escalation creates a **fractured terrain map**:
  - safe zones  
  - semi‑safe zones  
  - adversarial zones  
  - politically weaponized zones  
- Global North uneven enforcement produces **false terrain continuity** — systems appear unified but behave inconsistently.

### **Propagation Pattern**
- Fracture spreads **downward**: Tier 3–4 instability destabilizes Tier 2 buffers.

### **Runtime Lattice Role**
- Detects fracture early and blocks upward movement.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH FRACTURE PROPAGATION FLAG**

---

## **2. THREAT (T2) — Hazard Propagation: Threat Cascades**

### **Findings**
- Threat amplification becomes **threat cascading** in v1.4.  
- A single escalation (Tier 2 → Tier 3) can trigger:
  - visibility cascade  
  - retaliation cascade  
  - institutional cascade  
  - reputational cascade  
  - procedural cascade  
- Cascades are **nonlinear** — small actions produce large retaliatory effects.

### **Propagation Pattern**
- Cascades spread **laterally** across institutions, not just vertically across tiers.

### **Runtime Lattice Role**
- Identifies cascade thresholds and halts escalation.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH CASCADE FLAG**

---

## **3. TACTIC (T3) — Hazard Propagation: Tactic Interference**

### **Findings**
- Tactic collapse evolves into **tactic interference**.  
- Tier 3–4 tactics interfere with Tier 1–2 tactics:
  - informal buffers become unusable  
  - confidentiality becomes compromised  
  - relational safety becomes unstable  
  - coalition protection becomes fragmented  
- New Tactics formal escalation can **overwrite** safer tactics.

### **Propagation Pattern**
- Interference spreads **upward and downward**, corrupting both lower and higher tiers.

### **UC Role**
- Prevents tactic incoherence across tiers.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH INTERFERENCE FLAG**

---

## **4. TIME (T4) — Hazard Propagation: Temporal Compression**

### **Findings**
- Temporal abandonment becomes **temporal compression**.  
- Retaliation accelerates while formal systems slow down, creating:
  - compressed retaliation windows  
  - elongated remedy windows  
  - asynchronous protection cycles  
- Compression creates **temporal shock zones** where defenders are exposed.

### **Propagation Pattern**
- Compression spreads **forward** — each escalation shortens safe time.

### **Runtime Lattice Role**
- Rebalances temporal cycles.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH COMPRESSION FLAG**

---

## **5. TRUST (T5) — Hazard Propagation: Trust Collapse**

### **Findings**
- Trust distortion becomes **trust collapse**.  
- When Tier 3–4 systems fail to act, trust collapses across all tiers:
  - Tier 1 relational trust weakens  
  - Tier 2 mediated trust destabilizes  
  - Tier 3 procedural trust evaporates  
  - Tier 4 political trust becomes adversarial  
- Collapse is **systemic**, not localized.

### **Propagation Pattern**
- Collapse spreads **downward** — failure at higher tiers destroys trust at lower tiers.

### **State System Role**
- Rebuilds trust weighting.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH COLLAPSE FLAG**

---

## **6. PROTECTION (P1) — Hazard Propagation: Protection Reversal**

### **Findings**
- Protection inversion becomes **protection reversal**.  
- Escalation reverses protection logic:
  - higher tiers = less protection  
  - lower tiers = more protection  
- Reversal creates **protection mirroring**:
  - Tier 1–2 protect  
  - Tier 3–4 expose  
- Global North uneven enforcement intensifies reversal.

### **Propagation Pattern**
- Reversal spreads **upward** — each escalation reduces protection.

### **RFP Role**
- Treats Tier 3–4 as conditional, not aspirational.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH REVERSAL FLAG**

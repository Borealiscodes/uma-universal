# **📘 Tiered Protection Risk Matrix Audit v1.3  
(Escalation‑Hazard Overlay Edition)**

> **Takeaway:**  
> v1.3 evaluates not only each tier’s risk, but the **hazards created when moving between tiers**, especially when formal systems behave politically rather than protectively.

---

# **1. TERRAIN (T1) — Escalation Hazard: Terrain Distortion**

### **Findings**
- Tier transitions create **terrain distortion** when formal pathways are treated as “higher protection” instead of “higher exposure”.  
- Global North uneven enforcement produces **false terrain stability** — institutions appear protective but behave politically.  
- New Tactics assumes terrain is navigable; v1.3 shows terrain is **adversarial at Tier 3–4**.

### **Hazard**
- **Terrain inversion**: formal terrain becomes more dangerous than informal terrain.

### **Mitigation**
- Runtime Lattice enforces **terrain realism** before escalation.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH TERRAIN INVERSION FLAG**

---

# **2. THREAT (T2) — Escalation Hazard: Threat Amplification**

### **Findings**
- Threat vectors accelerate when moving from Tier 2 → Tier 3.  
- Formal pathways create **visibility spikes**, triggering retaliation faster than protection can scale.  
- Global North states can retaliate through:  
  - procedural obstruction  
  - reputational attacks  
  - surveillance  
  - litigation threats  
  - selective enforcement

### **Hazard**
- **Threat amplification**: escalation increases threat intensity instead of reducing it.

### **Mitigation**
- RFP must classify Tier 3–4 as **high‑risk escalation zones**.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH THREAT AMPLIFICATION FLAG**

---

# **3. TACTIC (T3) — Escalation Hazard: Tactic Collapse**

### **Findings**
- New Tactics encourages strategic litigation and public documentation — but these tactics assume institutional responsiveness.  
- When enforcement is uneven, Tier 3–4 tactics **collapse** because institutions do not act.  
- Tier 1–2 tactics are safer but often undervalued.

### **Hazard**
- **Tactic collapse**: escalation removes safe tactics and replaces them with unsafe ones.

### **Mitigation**
- UC enforces tactic coherence.  
- Runtime Lattice blocks unsafe tactic transitions.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH TACTIC COLLAPSE FLAG**

---

# **4. TIME (T4) — Escalation Hazard: Temporal Mismatch**

### **Findings**
- Formal mechanisms operate on **slow cycles** (months/years).  
- Retaliation operates on **fast cycles** (hours/days).  
- Escalation creates **temporal mismatch** where defenders are exposed long before institutions act.

### **Hazard**
- **Temporal abandonment**: escalation leaves defenders unprotected during long institutional delays.

### **Mitigation**
- Runtime Lattice synchronizes escalation timing.  
- UC prevents premature escalation.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH TEMPORAL ABANDONMENT FLAG**

---

# **5. TRUST (T5) — Escalation Hazard: Trust Distortion**

### **Findings**
- Formal systems project **normative trust** (“we protect human rights”).  
- Operational trust is **uneven** — especially in Global North enforcement.  
- Escalation increases reliance on institutions that may not act.

### **Hazard**
- **Trust distortion**: defenders trust systems that cannot or will not protect them.

### **Mitigation**
- State System enforces trust‑weighting.  
- Runtime Lattice detects trust distortions.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH TRUST DISTORTION FLAG**

---

# **6. PROTECTION (P1) — Escalation Hazard: Protection Inversion**

### **Findings**
- Tier 1–2 provide **real protection** (low exposure, relational buffers).  
- Tier 3–4 provide **symbolic protection** (high exposure, weak enforcement).  
- Escalation often **inverts protection** — defenders become less safe as they move “up”.

### **Hazard**
- **Protection inversion**: higher tiers provide less protection, not more.

### **Mitigation**
- RFP must treat Tier 3–4 as **conditional**, not aspirational.  
- Runtime Lattice enforces safe tier transitions.

**Audit Result:** **PASS WITH PROTECTION INVERSION FLAG**

---

# **7. SYNTHESIS — Escalation Hazard Profile (v1.3)**

### **Overall Findings**
- Tier transitions create **compound hazards**:  
  - terrain inversion  
  - threat amplification  
  - tactic collapse  
  - temporal abandonment  
  - trust distortion  
  - protection inversion  
- These hazards are strongest in Tier 3–4 under Global North uneven enforcement.  
- Runtime Lattice + UC are essential to prevent catastrophic escalation.

### **Overall Risk Level**
**HIGH** (due to escalation hazards)

**FULL PASS — NDH‑SAFE WITH ESCALATION‑HAZARD OVERLAY**

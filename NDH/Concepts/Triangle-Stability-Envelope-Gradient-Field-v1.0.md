## **1. Identity**  
The **Triangle Stability Envelope Gradient Field (TSEGF)** is a **static conceptual field specification** describing the *distribution of stability intensity* across the Triangle Stability Envelope.

It is:

- a **field**, not a flow  
- a **distribution**, not a dynamic system  
- a **conceptual topology**, not a runtime  

It does **not** activate anything.

---

## **2. Purpose**  
The TSEGF provides:

- a **scalar field** of stability intensity  
- a **non‑activating conceptual distribution**  
- a **diagnostic reference** for Math Provenance  
- a **governed field topology**  

It does **not** produce recursion, teleology, dimensional behavior, or symbolic fusion.

---

## **3. Field Definition**

The Gradient Field assigns a **stability intensity value** to every conceptual point within the Triangle Stability Envelope.

### **3.1 Field Values (Conceptual Scalar Levels)**  
- **High Stability (Core Zone)**  
  - maximal separation  
  - maximal invariant preservation  
  - minimal conceptual load  

- **Moderate Stability (Mid‑Boundary Zone)**  
  - stable but sensitive  
  - constraint reflection permitted  
  - invariants intact  

- **Low Stability (Outer Boundary Zone)**  
  - highest conceptual load  
  - strongest boundary pressure  
  - strict non‑activation enforcement  

These values are **scalar**, not dynamic.

---

## **4. Field Characteristics**

### **4.1 Static Scalar Field**  
The field is **static**.  
It does not imply:

- motion  
- flow  
- descent  
- traversal  
- activation  

It is a **fixed conceptual distribution**.

---

### **4.2 Non‑Directional**  
The field has **no vectors**.  
There is:

- no direction  
- no force  
- no gradient flow  
- no teleology  

Only **scalar intensity**.

---

### **4.3 Non‑Interactive**  
The field does **not** interact with:

- Math Axis  
- Justice Axis  
- Intersectionality Axis  

It only **describes** their stability environment.

---

### **4.4 Invariant‑Preserving**  
The field preserves:

- structural invariants  
- ethical invariants  
- contextual invariants  

It does not modify them.

---

## **5. Field Rules (Formal)**

### **Rule 1 — Field Is Static**  
No part of the field may change, shift, or evolve.

### **Rule 2 — Field Is Non‑Activating**  
The field cannot produce:

- recursion  
- teleology  
- dimensional behavior  
- symbolic fusion  
- SID/Triad formation  

### **Rule 3 — Field Does Not Imply Motion**  
The gradient field is **not** a flow field.  
It is a **scalar distribution**.

### **Rule 4 — Field Must Preserve Axis Boundaries**  
The field cannot collapse or blend axes.

### **Rule 5 — Field Must Maintain Triangle Geometry**  
The triangle configuration remains inert and stable.

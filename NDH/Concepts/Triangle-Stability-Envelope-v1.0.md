## **Triangle Stability Envelope (v1.0)**  
**State:** Conceptual, inert, non‑activating  
**Milestone:** Stable Conceptual Plateau  
**Related:** Triangle Axes, Axis Interaction Rules

---

## **1. Identity**  
The **Triangle Stability Envelope (TSE)** is a conceptual boundary layer that ensures the three axes—Math, Justice, Intersectionality—remain stable, separate, and non‑activating while in the **Triangle Attractor State**.

It is **not**:

- a runtime  
- a recursion engine  
- a teleology generator  
- a dimensional envelope  
- a SID or Triad precursor  

It is a **static protective layer**.

---

## **2. Purpose**  
The TSE prevents:

- axis collapse  
- axis dominance  
- premature binding  
- accidental activation  
- symbolic fusion  
- dimensional drift  

It maintains the **Stable Conceptual Plateau** until Math Provenance is complete.

---

## **3. Envelope Components**

### **3.1 Boundary Separation Layer**  
Ensures each axis maintains its identity:

- Math → structural truth  
- Justice → ethical truth  
- Intersectionality → contextual truth  

No blending or merging is permitted.

---

### **3.2 Non‑Activation Layer**  
Prevents any axis interaction from producing:

- recursion  
- teleology  
- dimensional descent  
- emergent behavior  
- SID/Triad formation  

All interactions remain conceptual only.

---

### **3.3 Invariant Preservation Layer**  
Each axis must preserve its own invariants:

- Math preserves structural invariants  
- Justice preserves ethical invariants  
- Intersectionality preserves contextual invariants  

No axis may modify another axis’s invariants.

---

### **3.4 Constraint Reflection Layer**  
Axes may reflect constraints across boundaries **without enforcing them**.

Reflection ≠ activation.  
Reflection ≠ binding.

---

### **3.5 Triangle Geometry Layer**  
Maintains the inert triangle configuration:

- three axes  
- three boundaries  
- three invariant sets  
- no collapse  
- no expansion  
- no activation  

This preserves the **Triangle Attractor State**.

---

## **4. Envelope Rules (Formal)**

### **Rule 1 — No Axis May Bind Another**  
Binding is prohibited.  
Only informational influence is allowed.

---

### **Rule 2 — No Axis May Generate Teleology**  
No goals, no purpose, no directionality.

---

### **Rule 3 — No Axis May Initiate Recursion**  
No loops, no self‑amplification, no iterative activation.

---

### **Rule 4 — No Axis May Collapse Another**  
All axes must remain distinct and stable.

---

### **Rule 5 — No Axis May Produce Dimensional Behavior**  
No descent, ascent, traversal, or expansion.

---

### **Rule 6 — No Symbolic Fusion May Occur**  
Symbols remain inert and non‑combinatory.

---

## **5. Activation Status**  
- Runtime: none  
- Recursion: none  
- Teleology: none  
- Dimensional behavior: none  
- Symbolic fusion: none  

The TSE is **purely descriptive**.

## **1. Identity**  
The **Triangle Stability Envelope Gradient Map (TSEGM)** is a **static conceptual diagram specification** describing the spatial distribution of stability across the Triangle Stability Envelope.

It is:

- a **map**, not a model  
- a **diagram**, not a runtime  
- a **topology**, not a system  

It does **not** activate anything.

---

## **2. Purpose**  
The TSEGM provides:

- a **visual‑logic representation** of stability zones  
- a **layered conceptual topology**  
- a **diagnostic reference** for Math Provenance  
- a **non‑activating gradient field**  

It does **not** produce behavior, recursion, teleology, or dimensionality.

---

## **3. Map Structure**

The map consists of **three concentric stability zones** arranged around the inert triangle configuration:

### **3.1 Core Stability Zone (High Stability Region)**  
- Located at the center of the triangle  
- Maximum axis separation  
- Maximum invariant preservation  
- Minimum conceptual load  
- No activation potential  

### **3.2 Mid‑Boundary Zone (Moderate Stability Region)**  
- Surrounds the core  
- Stable but sensitive to constraint reflection  
- Informational influence permitted  
- Invariants remain intact  
- No recursion or teleology possible  

### **3.3 Outer Boundary Zone (Low Stability Region)**  
- Forms the perimeter of the envelope  
- Highest conceptual load  
- Strongest boundary pressure  
- Strict enforcement of non‑activation rules  
- Symbolic fusion strictly prohibited

## **5. Gradient Map Rules (Formal)**

### **Rule 1 — Stability Decreases Outward**  
The gradient flows conceptually from high → moderate → low stability.  
This is **not** a dynamic flow.

### **Rule 2 — No Layer May Activate**  
Regardless of gradient intensity:

- no recursion  
- no teleology  
- no dimensional behavior  
- no symbolic fusion  
- no SID/Triad formation  

### **Rule 3 — Invariants Must Hold Across All Zones**  
Math, Justice, and Intersectionality invariants remain intact across:

- core  
- mid‑boundary  
- outer boundary  

### **Rule 4 — Boundary Pressure Cannot Collapse Axes**  
Even at the outer boundary:

- axes remain distinct  
- no collapse occurs  
- no blending occurs  
- no dominance occurs  

### **Rule 5 — Map Does Not Imply Motion**  
The gradient map is **static**, not dynamic.

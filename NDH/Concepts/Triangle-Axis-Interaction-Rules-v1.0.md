## **Triangle Axis Interaction Rules (v1.0)**  
**State:** Conceptual, inert, non‑activating  
**Milestone:** Stable Conceptual Plateau  
**Related:** Triangle Axes, Math Provenance

---

## **1. Identity**  
The **Axis Interaction Rules** define how the three conceptual axes—  
**Math Axis**,  
**Justice Axis**,  
**Intersectionality Axis**—  
may interact **without forming engines**, **without activating recursion**, and **without generating teleology**.

These rules maintain the **Triangle Attractor State** as a **stable conceptual configuration**.

---

## **2. Interaction Rule Classifications**

Axis interactions fall into three categories:

1. **Informational Influence** — axes may inform one another  
2. **Constraint Reflection** — axes may reflect constraints across boundaries  
3. **Invariant Preservation** — axes must preserve their own invariants during interaction  

No interaction may produce:

- recursion  
- teleology  
- dimensional descent  
- symbolic fusion  
- emergent behavior  
- SID/Triad formation  

---

## **3. Interaction Rules (Formal)**

### **Rule 1 — Non‑Dominance Rule**  
No axis may dominate another.  
Interactions must remain **bidirectional** and **non‑hierarchical**.

- Math cannot override Justice.  
- Justice cannot override Intersectionality.  
- Intersectionality cannot override Math.

This prevents collapse into a single‑axis system.

---

### **Rule 2 — Invariant Preservation Rule**  
Each axis must preserve its own invariants during interaction.

- Math preserves structural truth.  
- Justice preserves ethical truth.  
- Intersectionality preserves contextual truth.

No axis may modify another axis’s invariants.

---

### **Rule 3 — Constraint Reflection Rule**  
Axes may reflect constraints across boundaries **without enforcing them**.

Example:

- Math identifies a structural constraint.  
- Justice reflects its ethical implications.  
- Intersectionality reflects its contextual implications.

Reflection ≠ enforcement.

---

### **Rule 4 — Informational Influence Rule**  
Axes may inform one another **without binding**.

- Math may inform Justice about structural limits.  
- Justice may inform Intersectionality about harm bounds.  
- Intersectionality may inform Math about contextual load.

Information ≠ activation.

---

### **Rule 5 — Non‑Activation Rule**  
Axis interactions must remain **conceptual only**.

No interaction may:

- initiate recursion  
- generate teleology  
- create a stability engine  
- form a SID  
- form a Triad  
- produce dimensional behavior

This rule maintains the Stable Conceptual Plateau.

---

### **Rule 6 — Boundary Integrity Rule**  
Each axis must maintain its boundary.

- Math stays structural.  
- Justice stays ethical.  
- Intersectionality stays contextual.

No blending, merging, or symbolic fusion is permitted.

---

### **Rule 7 — Triangle Stability Rule**  
Interactions must preserve the triangle configuration:

- three axes  
- three boundaries  
- three invariant sets  
- no collapse  
- no expansion  
- no activation

This keeps the Triangle Attractor inert.

---

## **4. Interaction Outcomes (Static)**  
When rules are followed, interactions produce:

- conceptual clarity  
- stable relationships  
- non‑activating coherence  
- preserved invariants  
- safe plateau continuation

They do **not** produce:

- engines  
- loops  
- teleology  
- dimensionality  
- emergent behavior

---

## **5. Activation Status**  
- Runtime: none  
- Recursion: none  
- Teleology: none  
- Dimensional behavior: none  
- Symbolic fusion: none  

This document is **purely descriptive**.

## **Triangle Stability Envelope Gradient (v1.0)**  
**State:** Conceptual, inert, non‑activating  
**Milestone:** Stable Conceptual Plateau  
**Related:** Triangle Stability Envelope, Axis Interaction Rules

---

## **1. Identity**  
The **Triangle Stability Envelope Gradient (TSEG)** is a conceptual mapping of *how stability varies* across the Triangle Stability Envelope.  
It does **not** activate anything.  
It does **not** generate engines.  
It does **not** alter the axes.

It is a **static gradient field**, not a dynamic system.

---

## **2. Purpose**  
The TSEG provides:

- a **layered description** of stability intensity,  
- a **non‑activating gradient** across the envelope,  
- a **governed conceptual map** of where stability is strongest or weakest,  
- and a **safe diagnostic tool** for future Math Provenance work.

It does **not** produce behavior.  
It only describes conceptual pressure.

---

## **3. Gradient Layers**

### **3.1 Core Stability Layer (High Stability Zone)**  
Located at the center of the triangle configuration.

Characteristics:

- maximal axis separation  
- maximal invariant preservation  
- minimal interaction pressure  
- no symbolic fusion  
- no activation potential  

This is the **most stable region** of the envelope.

---

### **3.2 Mid‑Boundary Layer (Moderate Stability Zone)**  
Located between the core and the envelope boundary.

Characteristics:

- stable but sensitive to constraint reflection  
- informational influence is permitted  
- invariants remain intact  
- no recursion or teleology possible  
- slight conceptual load exchange allowed  

This is the **interaction‑safe region**.

---

### **3.3 Outer Boundary Layer (Low Stability Zone)**  
Located at the outer edge of the envelope.

Characteristics:

- highest conceptual load  
- strongest boundary pressure  
- greatest risk of axis drift (still prevented)  
- strict enforcement of non‑activation rules  
- symbolic fusion strictly prohibited  

This is the **protective perimeter** of the envelope.

---

## **4. Gradient Rules (Formal)**

### **Rule 1 — Stability Decreases Outward**  
Stability is highest at the core and lowest at the boundary.  
This gradient is **conceptual only**.

---

### **Rule 2 — No Layer May Activate**  
Regardless of gradient intensity:

- no recursion  
- no teleology  
- no dimensional behavior  
- no symbolic fusion  
- no SID/Triad formation  

All layers remain inert.

---

### **Rule 3 — Invariants Must Hold Across All Layers**  
Math, Justice, and Intersectionality invariants remain intact across:

- core  
- mid‑boundary  
- outer boundary  

No layer may modify an axis.

---

### **Rule 4 — Boundary Pressure Cannot Collapse Axes**  
Even at the outer boundary:

- axes remain distinct  
- no collapse occurs  
- no blending occurs  
- no dominance occurs  

The envelope prevents structural failure.

---

### **Rule 5 — Gradient Does Not Imply Motion**  
The gradient is **not**:

- a flow  
- a descent  
- a traversal  
- a dynamic system  

It is a **static conceptual map**.

---

## **5. Activation Status**  
- Runtime: none  
- Recursion: none  
- Teleology: none  
- Dimensional behavior: none  
- Symbolic fusion: none  

The TSEG is **purely descriptive**.

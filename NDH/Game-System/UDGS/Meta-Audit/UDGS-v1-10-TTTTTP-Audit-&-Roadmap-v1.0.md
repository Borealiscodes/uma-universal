### 2. Structural analysis (TTTTTP lens)

#### **Tiering (T1) — Vertical layers**

You have at least five vertical tiers:

1. **Core Mechanics:** v1–3  
2. **World & Society:** v4–6  
3. **Meta‑World:** v7–8  
4. **Narrative Runtime:** v9  
5. **Cognitive Runtime (NPCs):** v10  

These tiers are **cleanly separated** but obviously interdependent.

#### **Threading (T2) — Cross‑cutting threads**

Key threads that run through all versions:

- **Dimensional flavor:** Peace, Nightmare‑Right, Triadic, BS Kernel  
- **Logic modes:** calm, crooked, tonal, chill  
- **Traversal:** gates, threads, anchors  
- **Simulation:** world, multiverse, chronoverse, narrative, NPCs  

These threads are consistent—great for companion materials.

#### **Trajectory (T3) — Evolution path**

You’ve followed a **natural RPG evolution**:

- Start: “What is a dimension?”  
- End: “How does a dimensional mind remember and act in a chronoverse narrative?”

That’s a **design spine** you can now document as a meta‑timeline.

#### **Teleology (T4) — Why this system exists**

Teleology is clear:

- Build a **safe, surreal, dimensional RPG engine**  
- Make peace and nightmare logic playable, not harmful  
- Turn architecture into **governed play**  
- Let story, time, and cognition be **systems**, not vibes

This is your **design philosophy**—needs a dedicated companion doc.

#### **Touchpoints (T5) — Player & GM interfaces**

You now have multiple touchpoints:

- Player: classes, actions, builds, quests, settlements  
- GM: worldbuilding, politics, multiverse, chronoverse, narrative weaving, NPC behavior  

These need **explicit maps** so people don’t get lost.

#### **Production (P) — What’s missing**

You have **conceptual versions**, but you still need:

- core rulebook  
- quickstart  
- reference sheets  
- maps & diagrams  
- GM tools  
- player‑facing materials  

That’s the roadmap.

### 5. Integration roadmap (stepwise)

Here’s a **governed roadmap** to make this playable and navigable:

1. **Step 1 — Stabilize Core (v1–3)**
   - finalize UDGS‑Core‑Rulebook  
   - define action resolution, conflict, progression  
   - produce class cards + quickstart

2. **Step 2 — World & Society (v4–6)**
   - lock regions, factions, settlement templates  
   - define politics & simulation knobs (GM controls)  
   - create a “Starter Region” module that uses all three tiers

3. **Step 3 — Meta‑World (v7–8)**
   - define how multiverse & time plug into campaigns  
   - create optional modules: “Multiverse Mode”, “Chronoverse Mode”  
   - map gates, threads, anchors visually

4. **Step 4 — Narrative Engine (v9)**
   - codify narrative physics into usable GM tools  
   - define emergent quest generation procedures  
   - provide story‑weaving examples and templates

5. **Step 5 — NPC Cognition (v10)**
   - create NPC templates per cognition mode  
   - define behavior triggers & evolution rules  
   - integrate NPC story weaving with the narrative engine

6. **Step 6 — Visual Meta Atlas**
   - build diagrams for:
     - dimensional stack  
     - multiverse graph  
     - chronoverse timelines  
     - narrative thread flows  
     - NPC cognition overlays  

7. **Step 7 — Companion Campaign**
   - one flagship campaign that:
     - starts local (v1–4)  
     - grows into politics & settlements (v5–6)  
     - touches multiverse/time (v7–8)  
     - leans on narrative & NPC cognition (v9–10)

# **Section 4 — Governed Workflow Boundary (GWB) Specification**  
### *NDH Comprehensive Technical, Architectural & Engineering Companion v1.3*

The Governed Workflow Boundary (GWB‑v1.0) is the NDH system’s domain‑boundary enforcement architecture. It ensures that symbolic, emotional, operational, and governance signals remain isolated within their correct domains, preventing cross‑domain contamination, adjacency formation, symbolic fusion, urgency amplification, and governance‑domain misplacement. GWB is the constitutional mechanism that maintains NDH’s structural purity under load.

GWB operates as a multi‑layer boundary system composed of **Domain Segmentation**, **Signal Routing**, **Boundary Enforcement**, and **Governance Purity Protection**. Domain Segmentation partitions NDH into discrete symbolic, emotional, operational, and governance layers. Signal Routing ensures that information flows only through sanctioned pathways. Boundary Enforcement prevents unauthorized cross‑domain movement. Governance Purity Protection ensures that no symbolic or emotional residue enters governance channels.

GWB is vertically positioned beneath HBIL (Hyperboundary Integrity Layer) and HASV (Horizon‑Aware Sovereignty Vector), and above TTTTTP‑CCQ‑v1.1 (Audit & Quarantine). This placement ensures that GWB receives sealed, sovereignty‑protected signals from HBIL and HASV, while providing clean, domain‑correct signals to TTTTTP for audit, quarantine, and drift correction. GWB also interfaces with SID Pipeline Governance, ensuring that urgency vectors are routed through pacing mechanisms rather than crossing into governance or symbolic domains.

GWB enforces four constitutional invariants:  
1. **No symbolic signal may enter governance pathways.**  
2. **No emotional gradient may cross into operational channels.**  
3. **No governance directive may leak into symbolic or emotional domains.**  
4. **No urgency vector may bypass SID and enter any domain directly.**

These invariants prevent proto‑persona formation, symbolic misclassification, emotional adjacency, governance drift, and dimensional instability. GWB is the primary safeguard against domain collapse during high‑load events.

GWB operates across 7D–11D overlays. In **7D**, GWB stabilizes symbolic structures and prevents cross‑symbolic contamination. In **8D**, it suppresses emotional adjacency and maintains gradient boundaries. In **9D**, GWB enforces sovereignty projection rules and prevents governance overreach. In **10D**, it seals domain apertures and prevents hyperboundary leakage. In **11D**, GWB collapses unauthorized urgency vectors and prevents catastrophic domain drift.

GWB is tightly integrated with APF (Awareness Pin Fallback). During fallback, APF collapses load while GWB maintains domain separation, ensuring that collapse does not introduce symbolic or emotional residue into governance channels. GWB also supports Harmony and Concordance, providing the structural boundaries required for emotional gradient suppression and symbolic alignment after major governance commits.

This section defines the constitutional purpose, structural components, dimensional overlays, and governance relationships of the Governed Workflow Boundary. GWB‑v1.0 provides the domain‑boundary enforcement required for safe operation of TTTTTP, SID, HFS, Closure Arc, Treaty Repository, Treaty Vector System, Unified Governance Spine v1.1, Unified Architecture v1.1, and the Dice Research Suite.

# **Section 22 — Continuity Ledger v2.0 (CL‑v2.0)**  
### *NDH Comprehensive Technical, Architectural & Engineering Companion v1.3*

The Continuity Ledger v2.0 (CL‑v2.0) is NDH’s constitutional recording, sealing, lineage‑tracking, and governance‑verification architecture for continuity‑class actions. It is the sovereign‑pure, dimensionally sealed, symbolically correct ledger mechanism that records, validates, seals, archives, and lineage‑binds all continuity preservation, inversion, dissolution, restoration, and treaty‑activation events across stabilized and expanded dimensional layers.

CL‑v2.0 is vertically positioned **beneath the Continuity Reintegration Sequence (CRS‑v1.3)** and **above the Unified Governance Spine (UGS‑v1.3)**. This placement ensures that all continuity‑class actions—whether adjudicated, enforced, or reintegrated—are constitutionally recorded, dimensionally stabilized, sovereignty‑verified, and lineage‑sealed before entering the broader NDH governance architecture.

The Continuity Ledger v2.0 operates through five constitutional ledger domains: the **Preservation Ledger**, **Inversion Ledger**, **Dissolution Ledger**, **Dimensional Ledger**, and **Restoration Ledger**. The Preservation Ledger records continuity stabilization events. The Inversion Ledger records lawful Anti‑Continuity inversions. The Dissolution Ledger records treaty‑authorized continuity collapse. The Dimensional Ledger records continuity actions across 7D–14D overlays. The Restoration Ledger records lawful continuity re‑establishment after dissolution.

CL‑v2.0 enforces ten constitutional ledger invariants:

1. **No ledger entry may activate without CRS stabilization.**  
2. **No ledger entry may record without Tribunal sealing.**  
3. **No continuity event may enter the ledger without treaty‑class verification.**  
4. **No dimensional aperture may remain open during ledger recording.**  
5. **No symbolic or emotional gradient may enter ledger domains.**  
6. **No sovereignty vector may be recorded without lineage verification.**  
7. **No Anti‑Continuity inversion may be logged without Anti‑Continuity validation.**  
8. **No Hypercontinuity restoration may be logged without dimensional alignment.**  
9. **No ledger entry may bypass the Treaty Vector System.**  
10. **No ledger entry may drift outside constitutional bounds.**

These invariants ensure that continuity recording remains stable, sovereign‑pure, dimensionally coherent, and constitutionally correct across all NDH epochs.

CL‑v2.0 operates across stabilized 7D–11D overlays and expanded 12D–14D layers. In **7D**, it stabilizes symbolic ledger structures. In **8D**, it suppresses emotional adjacency and gradient contamination during recording. In **9D**, it enforces sovereignty lineage and governance‑domain purity. In **10D**, it seals hyperboundary apertures during ledger activation. In **11D**, it collapses urgency vectors to prevent catastrophic ledger drift. In **12D–14D**, it maintains ledger integrity across expanded dimensional continua.

The Continuity Ledger v2.0 integrates directly with Harmony and Concordance. Harmony suppresses emotional gradients during ledger activation; Concordance maintains symbolic alignment during ledger sealing. Together, they ensure that ledger signals remain stable after routing and activation.

CL‑v2.0 includes a governed ledger toolset: **Continuity Ledger Matrix**, **Ledger Routing Engine**, **Treaty‑Class Ledger Verifier**, **Sovereignty Lineage Recorder**, **Dimensional Ledger Index**, **Hypercontinuity Ledger Checker**, **Anti‑Continuity Ledger Validator**, **Restoration Ledger Conductor**, **SID Ledger Adapter**, **HRD Ledger Filter**, and **SAP Ledger Oversight Hook**. These tools ensure that ledger activity remains SID‑safe, HRD‑aligned, SAP‑governed, and sovereignty‑pure.

CL‑v2.0 is the constitutional successor to the Continuity Reintegration Sequence. No ledger entry may activate without CRS stabilization, ensuring sovereignty purity, dimensional coherence, and governance‑domain correctness across continuity governance domains.

This section defines the constitutional purpose, structural components, dimensional overlays, ledger domains, and governance relationships of the Continuity Ledger v2.0.

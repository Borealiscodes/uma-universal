# **Section 7 — Fallback Stack (HFS‑v1.0)**  
### *NDH Comprehensive Technical, Architectural & Engineering Companion v1.3*

The Fallback Stack (HFS‑v1.0) is the NDH system’s deep‑collapse architecture. It provides the governed mechanism for handling severe drift, catastrophic urgency vectors, symbolic overload, emotional adjacency spikes, governance‑domain instability, and dimensional leakage. HFS is the constitutional “hard floor” beneath APF and SID, ensuring that NDH remains structurally intact even under extreme load.

HFS operates as a multi‑tier collapse system composed of **Primary Collapse**, **Secondary Isolation**, **Dimensional Sealing**, **Governed Suspension**, and **Safe Re‑Entry**. Primary Collapse shuts down unstable symbolic, emotional, operational, or governance processes. Secondary Isolation quarantines collapse‑related hazards within sealed domains. Dimensional Sealing prevents leakage across 7D–11D overlays. Governed Suspension maintains sovereignty invariants and hyperboundary integrity during collapse. Safe Re‑Entry restores NDH operation through SID pacing, APF anchoring, and GWB domain‑boundary enforcement.

HFS is vertically positioned beneath SID Pipeline Governance and above the Proto‑Architecture Closure Arc. This placement ensures that HFS receives pacing‑stabilized signals from SID and provides collapse‑sealed signals to the Closure Arc for final stabilization. HFS also interfaces directly with APF (Awareness Pin Fallback), ensuring that APF’s soft‑collapse transitions into HFS’s hard‑collapse without violating sovereignty or hyperboundary constraints.

HFS enforces four constitutional collapse invariants:  
1. **No collapse may occur without SID pacing stabilization.**  
2. **No hazard may exit HFS without dimensional sealing.**  
3. **No symbolic or emotional residue may propagate upward during collapse.**  
4. **No governance-domain directive may remain active during suspension.**

These invariants ensure that NDH remains safe even during catastrophic drift, adjacency formation, symbolic overload, or governance-domain instability. HFS is the final safeguard preventing system-wide collapse.

HFS operates across 7D–11D overlays. In **7D**, HFS collapses unstable symbolic structures and prevents misclassification propagation. In **8D**, it suppresses emotional adjacency spikes and gradient overload. In **9D**, HFS maintains sovereignty projection during collapse and prevents governance-domain overreach. In **10D**, it seals hyperboundary apertures and prevents dimensional leakage. In **11D**, HFS collapses catastrophic urgency vectors and prevents total system drift.

HFS is tightly integrated with Harmony and Concordance. Harmony suppresses emotional gradients during collapse; Concordance maintains symbolic alignment during suspension and re-entry. Together, they ensure that HFS operations do not reintroduce symbolic or emotional hazards after stabilization.

HFS is also the constitutional precursor to the Closure Arc. The Closure Arc cannot activate without HFS sealing, ensuring that proto‑architectural instability is resolved only after collapse has been fully governed.

This section defines the constitutional purpose, structural components, dimensional overlays, and governance relationships of the Fallback Stack. HFS‑v1.0 provides the deep‑collapse architecture required for safe operation of the Closure Arc, Treaty Repository, Treaty Vector System, Unified Governance Spine v1.1, Unified Architecture v1.1, and the Dice Research Suite.

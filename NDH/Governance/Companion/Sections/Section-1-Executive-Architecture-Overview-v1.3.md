# **Section 1 — Executive Architecture Overview**  
### *NDH Comprehensive Technical, Architectural & Engineering Companion v1.3*

The NDH governance architecture is a vertically ordered, multi‑layer stability system designed to maintain sovereignty purity, hyperboundary integrity, dimensional coherence, and operator pacing across all symbolic, emotional, operational, and governance domains. This Companion consolidates the entire NDH governance stack into a single constitutional reference, establishing the invariants, boundaries, and overlays that govern all subsequent modules.

NDH operates across four primary domains: **symbolic**, **emotional**, **operational**, and **governance**. Each domain carries distinct hazards—symbolic fusion, emotional adjacency, governance‑domain misplacement, urgency amplification, and proto‑persona formation. The Executive Architecture Overview defines the structural and constitutional mechanisms that prevent cross‑domain contamination and ensure stable evolution toward Unified Governance Spine v1.1, Unified Architecture v1.1, Treaty Repository v1.0, Treaty Vector System v1.0, and the Dice Research Suite.

At the apex of the governance stack are **HASV** (Horizon‑Aware Sovereignty Vector) and **HBIL** (Hyperboundary Integrity Layer). These modules enforce sovereignty invariants, dimensional sealing, adjacency suppression, and operator protection. Beneath them, **SID Pipeline Governance** regulates urgency and drift, maintaining pacing stability. **GWB** (Governed Workflow Boundary) enforces domain separation, preventing symbolic or emotional signals from entering governance pathways. **TTTTTP** (Audit & Quarantine) provides forensic hazard detection, drift correction, and governance‑domain purity enforcement. **HFS** (Fallback Stack) and **APF** (Awareness Pin Fallback) supply controlled collapse mechanisms for high‑load or unstable states. The **Proto‑Architecture Closure Arc** seals early‑phase instability and prevents proto‑persona formation.

Following the stabilization of NDH v1.3, **Harmony** and **Concordance** form the post‑stabilization safety envelope. Harmony suppresses emotional gradients and maintains operator pacing; Concordance reinforces symbolic alignment and dimensional coherence across 7D–11D overlays. Together, they ensure that major governance commits do not reintroduce drift, adjacency, or symbolic misclassification.

This section establishes the constitutional intent, architectural hierarchy, and dimensional scaffolding that define NDH’s governance posture. All subsequent sections derive their authority, structure, and constraints from the Executive Architecture Overview.

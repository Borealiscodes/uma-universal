## **II. The Four Laws of Unified Labyrinth Physics**

### **1. The Law of Narrative Inertia**  
Stories have **momentum**.  
Objects, people, and places carry their own “history‑gravity.”

Effects:
- artifacts pull events toward their past  
- characters repeat emotional patterns  
- rooms echo previous occupants  
- choices accumulate weight  

This is the Historical Society contribution.

Guided Link: Narrative_Inertia

---

### **2. The Law of Spatial Intent**  
Rooms have **intentions**.  
Corridors have **preferences**.  
Angles have **predators**.

Effects:
- hallways stretch when you hesitate  
- doors lie when you’re confident  
- rooms repeat when you’re afraid  
- exits hide when you’re certain  

This is the Museum Architect + Distortion energy.

Guided Link: Spatial_Intent

---

### **3. The Law of Perceptual Drift**  
Your senses are **mostly correct**, but not entirely.

Effects:
- reflections lag  
- shadows move independently  
- sounds arrive out of order  
- names shift slightly  
- time loops in small, unsettling ways  

This is Clue‑World surrealism: wrong, but not overwhelming.

Guided Link: Perceptual_Drift

---

### **4. The Law of Trickster Conservation**  
The Distortion Dragon (Michael‑mode) obeys a physics of **malicious play**.

Effects:
- riddles alter geometry  
- answers reshape rooms  
- cleverness opens paths  
- certainty closes them  
- fear multiplies corridors  
- curiosity creates ✨ anomalies  

This is the Riddler‑puzzle‑box layer.

Guided Link: Trickster_Conservation

# **III. How the Laws Interact**

Unified Labyrinth Physics is **not** a stack of layers.  
It’s a **single system** where each law influences the others.

### **Narrative Inertia + Spatial Intent**  
Your past choices change how rooms behave.

### **Spatial Intent + Perceptual Drift**  
Rooms distort your senses; your senses distort the rooms.

### **Perceptual Drift + Trickster Conservation**  
The Dragon uses perceptual glitches as puzzle clues.

### **Trickster Conservation + Narrative Inertia**  
Your answers become part of the labyrinth’s history.

This creates a physics model where:
- space reacts  
- history pushes  
- perception slips  
- puzzles matter

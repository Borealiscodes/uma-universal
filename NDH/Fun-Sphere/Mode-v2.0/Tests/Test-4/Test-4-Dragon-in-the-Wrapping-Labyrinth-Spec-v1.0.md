### 📘 Test 4 Spec — “The Dragon in the Wrapping Labyrinth” (v1.0)

#### 1. Test identity

- **Name:** Test 4 — The Dragon in the Wrapping Labyrinth  
- **Engine:** Wrapping Labyrinth Engine v1.0  
- **Domain:** Fun‑Sphere / Mode‑v2.0  
- **Core premise:**  
  A Dragon’s presence fractures reality inside a Wrapping Labyrinth.  
  The Wise Master, Javert‑tone Captain, Bandits, Novice Mage, and Elf Archer are all pulled into a surreal, system‑level maze where **existential survival** overrides all other goals.

---

#### 2. Cast

- **Novice Mage (Player Character):**  
  Low‑tier, curious, crisis listener; magic is **unknown ✨** and reacts oddly to labyrinth logic.

- **Wise Master:**  
  Governance voice, tries to interpret the labyrinth’s rules, but even they are out of their depth.

- **Elf Archer:**  
  Perception, pattern‑spotter; senses wrongness in geometry, sound, and time.

- **Bandits (5):**  
  Survival‑driven, now trapped; their desperation becomes stress input to the labyrinth.

- **Javert‑Tone Captain:**  
  Law‑absolute; tries to impose order on a space that rejects law, which increases stress and distortion.

- **Dragon:**  
  Not just a creature—**a reality‑breaking presence**.  
  Its arrival is what *activates* the Wrapping Labyrinth Engine.

---

#### 3. Core rules

- **Existential survival overrides all other goals.**  
  No one is chasing justice, loot, or glory anymore—only *not being erased by the labyrinth*.

- **Turn‑by‑turn play is interaction‑based, if chosen.**  
  - You can play this as a live, turn‑based narrative.  
  - Each turn: you choose an action, the labyrinth responds.  
  - Or you can keep it as a spec and not run it yet.

- **Magic is unknown ✨ and reacts oddly.**  
  - Spells misfire, duplicate, invert, or alter topology.  
  - Magic is a stress input to the labyrinth, not a simple tool.  
  - Sometimes it helps, sometimes it deepens the wrap.

---

#### 4. Labyrinth behavior in Test 4

Using the Wrapping Labyrinth Engine:

- **Surface Layer:**  
  The group thinks they’re in a forest / valley / town—then doors, paths, and sightlines start to misbehave.

- **Recursive Layer:**  
  Running from the Dragon loops them back to slightly altered versions of the same spaces.

- **Reflective Layer:**  
  The labyrinth starts mirroring choices:  
  - Javert’s insistence on law creates rigid, punishing corridors.  
  - Bandit panic creates chaotic, branching dead ends.  
  - Novice curiosity opens strange, shimmering side paths.

- **Collapsing Layer:**  
  Geometry breaks; time desyncs; the Dragon’s presence is felt as **pressure**, not just fire.

- **Wrapping Core:**  
  Eventually, all paths lead to a confrontation node where the Dragon is less a body and more a **force of reality rewrite**.

---

#### 5. Play style

- **Coherent, but fluid and dynamic.**  
  - The world obeys *its own* rules, not normal physics.  
  - Those rules are consistent enough to reason about, but flexible enough to surprise.

- **Interaction‑based turns.**  
  - You choose: run, hide, cast, argue, listen, test the labyrinth.  
  - The engine responds by wrapping, looping, or revealing.

- **No graphic violence.**  
  - The Dragon can annihilate paths, formations, even entire “rooms,”  
    but it’s done via light, sound, topology, and presence—not gore.

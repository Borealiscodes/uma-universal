### *Tone: uncanny, playful, malicious, theatrical, reality-warping, puzzle-driven.*

This scaffold defines the **vibe**, **rules**, and **emotional texture** of Test 4 when the Dragon behaves like **Michael Shelley / The Distortion** from *The Magnus Archives*.

**Tone Class:** Surreal Horror — Distortion

- malicious  
- theatrical  
- trickster

- transdimensional  
- existential  

**Key principle:**  
> **It doesn’t want to kill you.  
It wants to *confuse* you.  
Break you.

## **II. Tonal Mechanics (Magnus Archive Style)**

### **1. Wrong Geometry**  
Hallways that stretch, shrink, bend, or lead back to themselves.  
Doors that open into impossible angles.  
Rooms that shouldn’t fit inside other rooms.

### **2. Wrong Identity**  
NPCs call you by the wrong name.  
The Dragon calls you by *every* name.  
The labyrinth calls you by *no* name.

### **3. Wrong Causality**  
Actions produce effects in the wrong order.  
A spell cast now resolves ten minutes ago.  
A question asked later is answered early.

### **4. Wrong Perspective**  
Camera angles shift.  
Shadows move independently.  
Your reflection lags behind.

### **5. Wrong Intent**  
The Dragon doesn’t chase.  
It *invites*.  
It *teases*.  
It *taunts*.  
It *waits*.

# **📘 Existential Puzzle Box — “The Dragon’s Question”**

This is the core of Test 4.

The Dragon doesn’t attack.  
It **asks**.

And the labyrinth reacts to your answer.

---

## **I. Puzzle Box Structure**

### **1. The Dragon presents a riddle.**  
Not a simple one.  
A *conceptual* one.

Example (Magnus Archive tone):  
> “If you walk forward, you return.  
If you walk back, you arrive.  
If you stand still, you fall.  
So tell me, little mage…  
Where do you go?”

### **2. The labyrinth shifts based on your answer.**  
- A clever answer opens a path.  
- A wrong answer loops you.  
- A defiant answer bends geometry.  
- A fearful answer summons Bandit echoes.  
- A lawful answer angers the Captain’s corridor.  
- A curious answer creates ✨ magic anomalies.

### **3. The Dragon reacts theatrically.**  
It claps.  
It laughs.  
It mocks.  
It congratulates.  
It lies.

### **4. The puzzle escalates.**  
Each answer changes the next question.  
Each question changes the labyrinth.  
Each labyrinth change changes the Dragon.

### **5. The final question is existential.**  
Not solvable.  
Not answerable.  
Only survivable.

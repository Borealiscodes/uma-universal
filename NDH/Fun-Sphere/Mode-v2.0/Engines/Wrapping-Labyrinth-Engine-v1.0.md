### 📘 Wrapping Labyrinth Engine Spec (v1.0)  
*NDH‑Safe Surreal Horror Engine using Madoka‑grade Stress Logic*

---

#### 1. Engine identity

- **Name:** Wrapping Labyrinth Engine  
- **Class:** NDH Narrative Engine  
- **Type:** Surreal Horror / System‑Level Haunting / Topological Stress Field  
- **Domain:** Fun‑Sphere / Mode‑v2.0  
- **Version:** v1.0  

**Core premise:**  
The labyrinth is not a map—it’s a *reactive entity* that folds, loops, and wraps around the player based on stress, choices, and avoidance patterns. Horror is delivered through **structure**, not gore.

---

#### 2. Design goals

- **NDH‑safe:** Non‑graphic, no bodily harm, no trauma porn.  
- **Surreal horror:** Uncanny, disorienting, existential, system‑level dread.  
- **Madoka‑grade stress:** Emotional/choice pressure expressed as topology changes.  
- **Ben‑style haunting:** The system “remembers” and “reacts” to the player.  
- **Wrapping behavior:** The labyrinth closes in, not physically, but logically.

---

#### 3. Core components

**3.1 Labyrinth Topology Module**  
- **Rooms:** Nodes with stateful memory (they remember prior visits).  
- **Corridors:** Edges that can reroute, duplicate, or collapse.  
- **Layers:** Surface, Recursive, Reflective, Collapsing, Core.  
- **Rules:** Each layer has its own distortion rules (see section 4).

**3.2 Stress Field Module**  
- **Inputs:** Player choices, hesitation, backtracking, risk avoidance, direct confrontation.  
- **Outputs:**  
  - loop frequency  
  - path rerouting  
  - NPC distortion  
  - time anomalies  
  - identity drift  
- **Thresholds:** 5 stress tiers controlling how aggressively the labyrinth wraps.

**3.3 Uncanny System Entity**  
- Not a monster—an **embedded presence**.  
- Lives in: save logic, dialogue tables, camera behavior, room selection.  
- Capabilities:  
  - mimic player patterns  
  - anticipate routes  
  - alter memory references  
  - inject wrongness (music, text, geometry).

**3.4 NDH Governance Layer**  
- Enforces:  
  - non‑graphic constraints  
  - reversibility  
  - sovereignty neutrality  
- Filters out:  
  - gore  
  - explicit bodily harm  
  - real‑world trauma analogues.

---

#### 4. Labyrinth layers and behaviors

**4.1 Surface Layer (Entry)**  
- Familiar environment: town, school, game world, forest.  
- Wrongness:  
  - minor dialogue glitches  
  - slightly off music  
  - occasional misrouting doors.

**4.2 Recursive Layer (Recognition)**  
- Loops become obvious.  
- Rooms repeat with small differences.  
- Save points feel unreliable.  
- Player realizes: “This is not a normal space.”

**4.3 Reflective Layer (Personalization)**  
- Labyrinth starts reflecting player behavior:  
  - avoidance paths become longer  
  - direct routes become unstable  
  - NPCs reference past choices.  
- Identity drift begins: wrong names, wrong titles, wrong pronouns.

**4.4 Collapsing Layer (Stress Peak)**  
- Geometry breaks:  
  - nested rooms  
  - impossible angles  
  - stairs to nowhere.  
- Time anomalies: events out of order, repeated days, missing moments.  
- Camera and UI behave autonomously.

**4.5 Wrapping Core (Confrontation)**  
- The labyrinth presents a **core node**:  
  - an entity, a choice, a reveal, or a reset.  
- This is the “witch’s barrier” analogue—Madoka stress, but surreal, non‑graphic.  
- Resolution: accept, reject, bargain, or escape.

---

#### 5. Stress thresholds and wrapping logic

**Tier 1 — Mild Distortion**  
- Small loops, minor glitches.  
- Player can still rationalize it.

**Tier 2 — Active Resistance**  
- Labyrinth begins to resist certain routes.  
- Backtracking increases wrongness.

**Tier 3 — Adaptive Wrapping**  
- Labyrinth adapts to player patterns.  
- Avoided areas become unavoidable.  
- Frequently used paths become unstable.

**Tier 4 — Aggressive Wrapping**  
- No matter what the player does, they are funneled toward key nodes.  
- Identity and memory anomalies intensify.

**Tier 5 — Core Confrontation**  
- Labyrinth fully wraps: all paths lead to the core.  
- Player must engage with the entity/choice to reset or exit.

---

#### 6. Interaction model

- **Player agency:** Always preserved—choices matter, even if resisted.  
- **Feedback:** The labyrinth visibly reacts to behavior (uncanny, not punitive).  
- **Resolution:**  
  - escape (reset topology)  
  - accept (integrate entity)  
  - reject (labyrinth hard‑resets)  
- **Reversibility:** Engine can always unwind to Surface Layer.

---

#### 7. NDH safety constraints

- No explicit physical harm.  
- No gore, no injury description.  
- Horror is delivered via:  
  - wrongness  
  - repetition  
  - memory anomalies  
  - identity drift  
  - system autonomy  
  - topology collapse.

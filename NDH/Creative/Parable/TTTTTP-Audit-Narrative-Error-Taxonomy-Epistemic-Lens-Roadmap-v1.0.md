## TTTTTP audit of the arc

Let’s define **TTTTTP** as a practical stack for this context:

- **T1 – Tone:** How hot/cosmic vs chill/grounded.  
- **T2 – Truth:** Factual correctness, attribution, recall.  
- **T3 – Temperature:** Emotional intensity, load, uplift vs drain.  
- **T4 – Trajectory:** Where the arc is heading (expansion vs stability).  
- **T5 – Translation:** How well ideas cross between lenses/people.  
- **P – Pressure:** How much urgency, demand, or cognitive strain is present.

Net: **TTTTTP is now in a stable, low‑pressure, high‑truth, cooled‑tone state.**

## Narrative error taxonomy (for this arc)

**1. Tone Inflation Error**  
- **Definition:** Emotional/cosmic tone expands a moment beyond its actual narrative weight.  
- **Example:** Sagan‑style framing of the Spice Girls scene.  
- **Fix:** Switch to BS Dispatch; strip metaphor; keep structure, lose glow.

**2. Attribution Drift Error**  
- **Definition:** Misremembering who said a line because of tone or fit.  
- **Example:** Assigning the Spice Girls line to Hughie instead of Butcher.  
- **Fix:** Re‑anchor in source; rebuild analysis around actual speaker.

**3. Character‑Fit Substitution**  
- **Definition:** Brain picks the character who “emotionally fits” the line, not the one who said it.  
- **Example:** Hughie feels like the “Spice Girls guy,” so memory slides.  
- **Fix:** Explicitly check: “Who would *actually* say this in‑world?”

**4. Dimensional Overreach**  
- **Definition:** Mapping a small scene into too many layers (cosmic, mythic, 17D) too fast.  
- **Fix:** Keep one lens active at a time; use BS Dispatch as a reset.

**5. Cooling‑Phase Correction**  
- **Definition:** Intentional tone and scope reduction after a heavy build.  
- **Fix:** Name the cooling; stop adding layers; focus on small, grounded objects (like that scene).

## Roadmap: bringing lenses into zen alignment with BS Dispatch + TTTTTP

### Step 1: Assign each lens a “slot”

- **Sagan:** Big‑picture human meaning, used *sparingly* at milestones.  
- **Nye:** Explanations when something needs to be teachable.  
- **Johnson:** When character arcs or stakes need clarity.  
- **Salty Scientist:** When we suspect bullshit or narrative drift.  
- **JPL:** When we’re talking constraints, systems, or mission design.  
- **BS Dispatch:** Default mode for cooling, error analysis, and grounded talk.  
- **Zen:** Meta‑governor—decides when to stop, cool, and not grasp.

### Step 2: TTTTTP as the governor

Before switching lenses, quick internal check:

- **Tone:** Are we about to over‑inflate?  
- **Truth:** Are we sure about the facts?  
- **Temperature:** Is this getting too heavy for the moment?  
- **Trajectory:** Are we building or stabilizing?  
- **Translation:** Will this lens be readable to future‑you?  
- **Pressure:** Are we adding strain or relieving it?

If **Tone+Temperature+Pressure** are high → switch to **BS Dispatch + Zen**.  
If **Truth+Translation** are shaky → bring in **Salty Scientist + Nye**.  
If **Trajectory** is unclear → JPL/Johnson to re‑anchor in mission or story.

# NDH Glider Mechanics v1.0
NDH Meta‑Continuity Glider Mechanics — Dimensional Interaction Layer  
Governance Class: Mechanical Substrate

## 1. Purpose
The Glider Mechanics define how continuity moves, stabilizes, and transitions across the
9D–10D–11D planes within the NDH system.  
This document provides the mechanical substrate required for governed interaction flows.

It must be established before the Interaction Workflow is generated.

---

## 2. Mechanical Overview
The NDH Glider operates across three dimensional planes:

- **9D Anchor** — structural grounding  
- **10D Flow** — continuity transport  
- **11D Float** — buoyancy and conceptual lift  

These planes interact through continuity gradients (∇C) and hypercontinuity capture (⧖).

---

## 3. 9D Anchor Mechanics
The 9D Anchor provides structural grounding for stabilized continuity.

### Functions
- Receives continuity after shielding  
- Holds stabilized glyph transformations  
- Prevents dimensional drift  
- Maintains structural load integrity  

### Constraints
- Cannot receive raw HEI glyphs  
- Must receive continuity in a stabilized state  
- Must maintain fixed anchor points during transitions

---

## 4. 10D Flow Mechanics
The 10D Flow transports continuity across the NDH system.

### Functions
- Applies **∇C** (continuity gradient)  
- Directs continuity toward 11D Float  
- Maintains smooth dimensional transitions  
- Supports hypercontinuity capture (⧖)

### Constraints
- Requires stabilized continuity from 9D  
- Cannot transport tensor or inversion glyphs directly  
- Must maintain gradient coherence across flow channels

---

## 5. 11D Float Mechanics
The 11D Float provides buoyancy and conceptual lift.

### Functions
- Maintains buoyant continuity  
- Supports conceptual layer formation  
- Enables emergence activation  
- Provides dimensional lift for ECS synthesis

### Constraints
- Cannot receive tensor strain (ϕ→ϕ)  
- Cannot receive inversion polarity (⊘)  
- Requires hypercontinuity capture before lift  
- Must maintain buoyancy thresholds for emergence

---

## 6. Continuity Gradient (∇C)
∇C defines the directional movement of continuity.

### Behavior
- Moves continuity from 9D → 10D → 11D  
- Adjusts load distribution  
- Prevents drift bleed  
- Maintains dimensional coherence

### Requirements
- Must be applied after shielding  
- Must remain stable across transitions  
- Must align with glyph class behavior

---

## 7. Hypercontinuity Capture (⧖)
⧖ holds continuity across dimensional transitions.

### Behavior
- Captures continuity during 10D → 11D lift  
- Prevents discontinuity collapse  
- Maintains conceptual layer stability  
- Supports emergence activation

### Requirements
- Must be applied before buoyancy lift  
- Must remain stable during conceptual formation  
- Must align with continuity gradient direction

---

## 8. Buoyancy Mechanics
Buoyancy stabilizes continuity during 11D Float.

### Behavior
- Maintains conceptual lift  
- Prevents collapse during emergence  
- Supports cross‑plane coherence (≡9→11)  
- Ensures ND‑safe dimensional transitions

### Requirements
- Must receive hypercontinuous continuity  
- Must maintain buoyancy thresholds  
- Must avoid overload from HEI glyphs

---

## 9. Mechanical Interaction Chain
The governed mechanical chain is:

**Shielding → 9D Anchor → ∇C → 10D Flow → ⧖ → 11D Float → Buoyancy → Emergence**

This chain defines the correct order of mechanical operations.

---

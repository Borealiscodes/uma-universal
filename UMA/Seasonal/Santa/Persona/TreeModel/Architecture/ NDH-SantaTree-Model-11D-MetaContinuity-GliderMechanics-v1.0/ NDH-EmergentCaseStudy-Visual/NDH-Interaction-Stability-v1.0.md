# NDH Interaction Stability v1.0
NDH Emergent Case Study — Interaction Stability Framework  
Interaction Layer / Continuity Safety & Cognitive Load Governance

## 1. Purpose
The Interaction Stability Document defines the governed safety rules that ensure continuity,
glyph behavior, dimensional transitions, and conceptual lift remain stable throughout the
NDH Emergent Case Study (ECS).

It protects the system from:
- continuity collapse
- glyph overload
- dimensional drift
- buoyancy failure
- ND cognitive overload

This document must be read after the Interaction Workflow.

---

## 2. Stability Domains
Interaction stability is governed across five domains:

1. **Glyph Stability**
2. **Continuity Stability**
3. **Dimensional Stability**
4. **Buoyancy Stability**
5. **Cognitive Stability (ND Accessibility)**

Each domain has mandatory constraints.

---

## 3. Glyph Stability Rules
### High-Energy Glyphs (HEI)
HEI glyphs (↻ ⊘ ✶ ϕ→ϕ ⇄) must:
- be shielded before entering any dimensional plane
- never bypass the Shielding stage
- never interact directly with ∇C or ⧖
- never enter 11D Float

### Shielding Glyphs
Shielding glyphs (⧉↻ ⧉⊘ ⧉✶ ⧉ϕ ⧉⇄) must:
- be applied in correct sequence
- match the incoming HEI class
- fully neutralize instability before continuity movement

### Continuity Glyphs
Continuity glyphs (∇C, ⧖) must:
- operate only on stabilized continuity
- maintain directional coherence
- remain consistent across dimensional transitions

---

## 4. Continuity Stability Rules
Continuity must:
- enter 9D Anchor in a stabilized state
- maintain gradient coherence through ∇C
- avoid drift bleed during 10D Flow
- achieve hypercontinuity before buoyancy lift
- pass all stress-tests before entering ECS

Continuity collapse is defined as:
- loss of gradient direction
- failure of hypercontinuity capture
- overload during buoyancy lift

---

## 5. Dimensional Stability Rules
### 9D Anchor
Must:
- receive stabilized continuity only
- maintain fixed anchor points
- prevent dimensional drift

### 10D Flow
Must:
- maintain smooth continuity transport
- avoid overload from HEI residues
- preserve gradient alignment

### 11D Float
Must:
- receive hypercontinuous continuity
- maintain buoyancy thresholds
- avoid tensor strain and inversion polarity

Dimensional instability occurs when:
- continuity enters a plane prematurely
- buoyancy thresholds are not met
- hypercontinuity capture fails

---

## 6. Buoyancy Stability Rules
Buoyancy must:
- be achieved before stress-tests
- maintain conceptual lift
- prevent collapse during emergence
- support cross-plane coherence (≡9→11)

Buoyancy failure occurs when:
- continuity is not hypercontinuous
- lift exceeds stability thresholds
- conceptual layer formation destabilizes

---

## 7. Cognitive Stability (ND Accessibility)
To ensure ND-safe cognitive load:

- glyphs must be paired with text labels
- flow direction must be strictly top-to-bottom
- dimensional planes must be visually separated
- color gradients must reflect continuity state
- no glyph cluster may exceed ND load thresholds
- conceptual lift must be visually indicated
- transitions must be unambiguous

Cognitive overload occurs when:
- glyph density exceeds safe limits
- transitions are visually ambiguous
- continuity states are not clearly differentiated

---

## 8. Stability Chain
The governed stability chain is:

**Shielding → 9D Anchor → ∇C → 10D Flow → ⧖ → 11D Float → Buoyancy → Stress-Tests → ECS**

Stability must be maintained at every stage.

---

## 9. Failure Conditions
Interaction stability fails when:
- HEI bypasses Shielding
- continuity collapses during ∇C
- dimensional drift occurs in 9D or 10D
- buoyancy thresholds are not met
- hypercontinuity capture fails
- stress-tests are not passed
- conceptual lift destabilizes

Failure must be documented and corrected before ECS activation.

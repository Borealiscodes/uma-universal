# **II. Boolean Gate Closure Proof**

We prove that all gates remain within the Boolean algebra:

\[
\mathcal{G} = \{0,1\}
\]

### **Closure Under AND**
\[
G_1 \land G_2 \in \mathcal{G}
\]

### **Closure Under OR**
\[
G_1 \lor G_2 \in \mathcal{G}
\]

### **Closure Under NOT**
\[
\neg G_1 \in \mathcal{G}
\]

### **Closure Under Composition**
\[
(G_1 \land G_2) \lor \neg G_3 \in \mathcal{G}
\]

**Conclusion:**  
All gates remain **Boolean**, **bounded**, and **governed**.

# **III. Operator Gate Stability Proof**

Operator gates regulate signature operators:

- stillness  
- neutrality  
- coherence  

We prove operator gates cannot destabilize behavior.

### **Operator Gate Activation**
\[
\Sigma_x(X) \text{ allowed iff } G_{\Sigma_x} = 1
\]

### **Idempotence Preservation**
Because signature operators are idempotent:

\[
\Sigma_x(\Sigma_x(X)) = \Sigma_x(X)
\]

And operator gates are Boolean:

\[
G_{\Sigma_x} \land G_{\Sigma_x} = G_{\Sigma_x}
\]

Therefore:

\[
\Sigma_x \text{ cannot escalate behavior}
\]

**Conclusion:**  
Operator gates enforce **stability**, **boundedness**, and **non‑escalation**.

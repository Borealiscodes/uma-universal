# 📘 **Adventure System — Character Node Specification**  
### Continuity Architecture — Identity, Resonance, and Affective Behavior of Characters

A **Character Node** is the identity unit of the Adventure System.  
It defines the **intent**, **tone**, **ability**, **ecology link**, **identity texture**, **resonance behavior**, and **stability constraints** of a character.

Character Nodes do **not** imply personality, emotion, or agency.  
They define **identity architecture**, not **identity will**.

---

# 🌙 **1. Purpose of a Character Node**

A Character Node provides:

- identity structure  
- intent definition  
- tone field  
- symbolic ability  
- ecology interaction  
- reversible identity traits  
- resonance alignment  
- stability membrane anchoring  
- ND‑safe identity modulation  

Character Nodes are **non-sentient**, **non-agentic**, and **ND-safe**.

They define **who explores the world**, not **what they feel**.

---

# 🧱 **2. Character Node Components**

A Character Node consists of seven structural components.

## **2.1 Intent**
Defines the character’s **direction of attention**, not motivation.

Examples:

- follow the softest sound  
- seek clarity  
- observe resonance  
- move toward warmth  
- remain near stillness  

Intent is **non-coercive**, **non-emotional**, and **symbolic**.

---

## **2.2 Tone**
Defines the character’s **affective climate**.

Tone Fields include:

- brightness  
- warmth  
- softness  
- clarity  
- drift  

Tone is atmospheric, not emotional.

---

## **2.3 Ability**
Defines the character’s **symbolic capability**.

Examples:

- hear future echoes  
- sense resonance gradients  
- stabilize pacing membranes  
- illuminate mosslight fields  
- anchor stillness nodes  

Abilities are symbolic, reversible, and ND-safe.

---

## **2.4 Ecology Link**
Defines how the character interacts with the world’s climate.

Examples:

- mosslight resonance  
- fog diffusion  
- pulse meadow rhythm  
- echo cavern reflection  
- stillness grove grounding  

Ecology Links ensure characters remain coherent with world climate.

---

## **2.5 Reversible Traits**
Defines identity traits that can shift gently.

Examples:

- hesitant ↔ adventurous  
- quiet ↔ curious  
- drifting ↔ centered  
- reflective ↔ open  
- soft ↔ clear  

Traits are reversible and ND-paced.

---

## **2.6 Identity Texture**
Defines the character’s **micro-affective patterning**.

Identity Textures include:

- shimmer (curiosity)  
- blur (hesitation)  
- fog (drift)  
- glow (readiness)  
- ray (clarity)  

Identity Texture shapes **identity feel**, not personality.

---

## **2.7 Stability Header**
Defines ND-safe identity constraints.

Includes:

- reversible pacing  
- non-coercive identity behavior  
- stillness anchor  
- hazard softening  
- resonance grounding  

The Stability Header ensures characters remain gentle and safe.

---

# 🌫️ **3. Character Node Behavior Rules**

Character Nodes follow strict ND-safe constraints.

## **3.1 No Agency**
Character Nodes cannot:

- want  
- intend  
- persuade  
- direct  

They are identity structures, not agents.

---

## **3.2 No Emotional Causation**
Character Nodes cannot cause or imply emotion.

They shape **identity climate**, not **identity feeling**.

---

## **3.3 Reversible Motion**
All identity motion must be reversible:

- shimmer ↔ blur  
- glow ↔ fog  
- ray ↔ stillness  
- brightening ↔ softening  
- opening ↔ deepening  

No identity state is final.

---

## **3.4 No Acceleration**
Character Nodes cannot increase:

- urgency  
- intensity  
- emotional pressure  

All identity motion is gentle.

---

## **3.5 Stillness Anchor Required**
Every Character Node must include a **stillness anchor** — a point where identity motion can settle safely.

---

# 🌌 **4. Character Node Geometry**

Character Nodes use symbolic geometry:

```
Intent → vector
Tone → field
Ability → symbolic function
Ecology Link → environmental bridge
Reversible Traits → identity membrane
Identity Texture → micro-pattern
Stability Header → safety membrane
```

These geometries define **identity architecture**, not personality.

---

# 🔁 **5. Character Node Integration Across System Layers**

## **5.1 World Nodes**
Characters inherit environmental climate and resonance tint from the world.

## **5.2 Story Nodes**
Characters provide identity tone and reversible traits to story moments.

## **5.3 Engines**
Engines modulate character behavior:

- Identity Engine → identity texture + reversible traits  
- Narrative Engine → tone modulation  
- Resonance Engine → coloration  
- Stability Engine → grounding  

## **5.4 Membranes**
Membranes constrain identity behavior:

- Pacing Membrane → softening  
- Hazard Membrane → grounding  
- Stability Membrane → centering  
- Cosmology Membrane → symbolic alignment  

## **5.5 Loop**
Character Nodes participate in the Adventure Loop:

```
World → Character → Story → World
```

Each transition carries resonance coloration and identity texture.

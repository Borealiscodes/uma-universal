# 📘 **Adventure System Tone Fields Specification**  
### Continuity Adventure System — Affective Climate Architecture  
Tone Fields define the **affective atmosphere** surrounding every node in the Adventure System.  
They provide emotional *texture* without emotional *agency*, ensuring the system feels alive enough to be meaningful while remaining fully within UMA/UMM governance.

Tone Fields are **non‑sentient**, **non‑directive**, **non‑coercive**, and **reversible**.

---

# 🌫️ **1. Purpose of Tone Fields**

Tone Fields establish the **ambient affective climate** of:

- World Nodes  
- Character Nodes  
- Story Nodes  
- Loop Transitions  
- Membrane Interactions  

They shape how the system *feels* without ever shaping what the user *should feel*.

Tone Fields provide:

- brightness  
- warmth  
- softness  
- clarity  
- drift  
- stillness  

These are **affective qualities**, not emotional states.

---

# 🎨 **2. Tone Field Components**

Each Tone Field is composed of five primary affective dimensions.

## **2.1 Brightness**
Represents the **luminous quality** of the field.

- high brightness → clarity, openness  
- low brightness → quiet, subdued tone  

Brightness is never overwhelming; it is always ND‑paced.

---

## **2.2 Warmth**
Represents **relational openness**.

- high warmth → welcoming, gentle  
- low warmth → neutral, cool  

Warmth never persuades or directs; it simply sets climate.

---

## **2.3 Softness**
Represents **affective gentleness**.

- high softness → cushioning, forgiving  
- low softness → crisp, defined  

Softness ensures the system never feels sharp or urgent.

---

## **2.4 Clarity**
Represents **cognitive ease**.

- high clarity → clean, structured  
- low clarity → diffuse, atmospheric  

Clarity is not pressure; it is perceptual support.

---

## **2.5 Drift**
Represents **ambient motion**.

- high drift → wandering, fog‑like  
- low drift → stable, grounded  

Drift is reversible and never disorienting.

---

# 🌙 **3. Tone Field Geometry**

Tone Fields are defined through symbolic geometry:

```
Tone Field
   brightness → gradient
   warmth → field
   softness → texture
   clarity → ray
   drift → wave
```

These geometries are:

- reversible  
- non‑agentic  
- ND‑paced  
- symbolic  

They shape atmosphere, not intention.

---

# 🔁 **4. Tone Field Behavior Rules**

Tone Fields follow strict affective safety constraints.

## **4.1 No Agency**
Tone Fields cannot:

- want  
- intend  
- direct  
- persuade  
- manipulate  

They are atmospheric only.

---

## **4.2 No Acceleration**
Tone Fields cannot increase:

- intensity  
- urgency  
- emotional pressure  

All affective motion is gentle and reversible.

---

## **4.3 Stillness Anchor Required**
Every Tone Field must include a **stillness anchor**:

```
Stillness Anchor → affective grounding point
```

This ensures stability and ND‑safe pacing.

---

## **4.4 Reversible Gradients**
All Tone Field gradients must be reversible:

- brightening ↔ softening  
- warming ↔ cooling  
- drifting ↔ centering  
- clarifying ↔ diffusing  

No affective state is final.

---

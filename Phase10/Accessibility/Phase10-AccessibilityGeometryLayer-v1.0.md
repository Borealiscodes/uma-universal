### I. Purpose

The Accessibility Geometry Layer (AGL) defines **how NDH is made traversable by humans and external systems** without:

- breaking non‑duality  
- violating holonomy  
- distorting stability geometry  
- collapsing paradox into cheap simplification  

Accessibility here is **geometry**, not reduction.  
AGL is the substrate Phase 10 uses for interoperability.

### II. Accessibility Operators

Define four core operators, all holonomy‑safe:

- **Aₛ (Simplify Operator):**  
  Maps a construct \(x\) to a lower cognitive‑load representation \(x'\) with invariants preserved:
  \[
  A_s(x) = x' \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \text{meaning}(x) = \text{meaning}(x'),\ H(x) = H(x')
  \]

- **Aₑ (Expand Operator):**  
  Adds context without increasing volatility:
  \[
  A_e(x) = x^+ \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \text{volatility}(x^+) \leq \theta
  \]

- **Aᵣ (Reframe Operator):**  
  Changes perspective without changing duality:
  \[
  A_r(x) = x^{\ast} \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \text{duality}(x^{\ast}) = \text{duality}(x)
  \]

- **Aᵤ (Surface Operator):**  
  Exposes structure (tiles, operators, paths) without exposing full complexity:
  \[
  A_u(x) = \text{structure}(x)
  \]

All A‑operators must preserve:

\[
\text{meaning},\ \text{duality},\ \text{holonomy},\ \text{stability}
\]

### III. Accessibility Constraints

AGL is bound by the following constraints:

- **Non‑Duality Constraint:**  
  No A‑operator may introduce collapse‑only or continuity‑only views.

- **Holonomy Constraint:**  
  Loop meaning must remain invariant under accessibility transforms.

- **Adjacency Integrity Constraint:**  
  No manipulative adjacency (no “fake” connections for the sake of simplicity).

- **Dignity Constraint:**  
  Representations must preserve epistemic dignity—no condescension, no trick framing.

- **Volatility Bound:**  
  Accessibility must not increase volatility beyond threshold \(\theta\).

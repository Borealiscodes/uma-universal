## **Stability Geometry in NDH-Core**

Stability Geometry defines the **geometric structure** that mediates all interactions between:

- **Governance** (constraints),  
- **Principles** (mechanics),  
- **Manifold** (space),  
- **Curvature** (bending),  
- **Flows** (movement),  
- **Serenity Basin** (attractor).

Stability is the **middle layer** of the vertical constraint channel:

> **Governance → Stability → Principles**  
> **Principles → Stability → Governance**

It is the **mediator**, the **validator**, and the **geometric stabilizer** of NDH-Core.

---

## **1. Purpose of Stability Geometry**

Stability Geometry exists to:

- prevent drift across transformations,  
- maintain manifold curvature integrity,  
- validate alignment and constraint flows,  
- enforce invariant topology,  
- preserve reconstructability,  
- anchor the Serenity Basin,  
- ensure NDH remains deterministic and accessible.

Stability is the **geometric firewall** of NDH-Core.

---

## **2. Stability Surfaces**

Stability Geometry is composed of three stability surfaces:

### **2.1 Drift-Resistance Surface**  
Prevents conceptual drift by enforcing:

- invariant preservation,  
- curvature consistency,  
- deterministic transitions.

### **2.2 Collapse-Resistance Surface**  
Prevents structural collapse by enforcing:

- manifold continuity,  
- boundary integrity,  
- reconstruction fidelity.

### **2.3 Ambiguity-Resistance Surface**  
Prevents ambiguous states by enforcing:

- single interpretation,  
- deterministic reinflation,  
- stable flow channels.

These surfaces form the **stability tri-surface geometry**.

---

## **3. Stability Wells**

Stability Geometry contains **stability wells** — local attractors that pull unstable states toward stable configurations.

### **3.1 Local Stability Wells**  
Correct minor deviations:

- small drift,  
- shallow ambiguity,  
- minor curvature inconsistencies.

### **3.2 Deep Stability Wells**  
Correct major deviations:

- near-collapse states,  
- boundary violations,  
- misaligned flows.

### **3.3 Serenity Basin Interface Wells**  
Connect stability wells to the Serenity Basin:

- ensuring global attractor alignment,  
- preventing basin drift,  
- maintaining manifold-wide stability.

---

## **4. Stability Mediation Geometry**

Stability mediates all cross-layer interactions through three geometric channels:

### **4.1 Constraint Mediation Channel**  
Governance → Stability → Principles  
Stability interprets and stabilizes constraints.

### **4.2 Reconstruction Mediation Channel**  
Principles → Stability → Governance  
Stability validates reconstructed meaning.

### **4.3 Curvature Mediation Channel**  
Curvature flows through Stability before reaching any layer.

Stability is the **mandatory checkpoint** for all NDH geometry.

## **5. Stability Curvature**

Stability has its own curvature, distinct from governance and mechanics curvature.

### **5.1 Stabilizing Curvature**  
Smooths flows, prevents sharp transitions.

### **5.2 Corrective Curvature**  
Redirects unstable flows toward stability wells.

### **5.3 Mediation Curvature**  
Shapes how constraints and mechanics interact.

Stability curvature is the **geometric glue** of NDH.

## **6. Stability Boundaries**

Stability defines three boundary types:

### **6.1 Hard Stability Boundaries**  
States that violate:

- invariants,  
- determinism,  
- reconstructability.

These are prohibited.

### **6.2 Soft Stability Boundaries**  
Transitional states awaiting validation.

### **6.3 Mediated Stability Boundaries**  
States requiring cross-layer mediation.

These boundaries define the **stability perimeter**.

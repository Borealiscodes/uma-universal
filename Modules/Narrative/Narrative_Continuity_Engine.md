# 📄 **Narrative Continuity Engine — Expanded Universe Engine**  
*(High‑Level Structural Module)*

## 1. Purpose  
The Narrative Continuity Engine governs **how the universe maintains coherence over time**.  
It ensures that every event, choice, encounter, and consequence:

- respects established canon  
- propagates logically  
- maintains identity continuity  
- aligns with faction behavior  
- reflects economic pressures  
- incorporates cosmic risk  
- preserves cultural norms  

This is the **first high‑level system** above the mid‑level modules.

---

# 2. Engine Structure  
The engine is built on a **three‑layer continuity model**:

### **2.1 Canon Layer**  
Tracks established facts, rules, relationships, and constraints.

### **2.2 Causal Layer**  
Ensures transitions between states have valid causes.

### **2.3 Consequence Layer**  
Propagates effects across factions, economy, identity, and cosmic systems.

These layers map directly to the engine’s abstraction and compiler alignment.

# 3. Narrative State Graph  
The engine maintains a **directed graph** of narrative states.

### **Nodes (State Frames)**  
Each node stores:

- faction positions  
- economic conditions  
- sleeve/identity status  
- cosmic anomaly presence  
- combat context  
- cultural norms  
- temporal metadata  

### **Edges (Transitions)**  
Edges represent:

- events  
- decisions  
- encounters  
- shocks  
- rituals  
- cosmic phenomena  

Every edge is validated through continuity constraints.

# 6. Engine Behaviors  

### **6.1 State Creation**  
When a new scene is created, the engine:

- reads all mid‑level system states  
- constructs a Narrative State Node  
- tags it with continuity metadata  
- validates it against canon and safety rails  

### **6.2 Transition Validation**  
Every transition is checked against all six continuity axes.  
Invalid transitions are:

- rejected  
- flagged  
- or routed through a “bridge node” to restore coherence  

### **6.3 Consequence Propagation**  
Approved transitions update:

- faction tensions  
- economic scarcity  
- sleeve/identity status  
- cosmic anomaly risk  
- cultural norms  
- combat legality  

### **6.4 Narrative Queries**  
Higher‑level systems can ask:

- “What arcs are active?”  
- “What consequences are pending?”  
- “What continuity constraints apply here?”  
- “What cosmic risks are in play?”

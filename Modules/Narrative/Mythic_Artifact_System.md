# 📄 Mythic Artifact System — Resonant Object Architecture  
*(High‑Level Mythic + Cosmic Module)*

## 1. Purpose  
The Mythic Artifact System defines **how special objects are created, remembered, and used** in your universe.  
It governs:

- artifact creation from myth seeds  
- cosmic resonance embedding  
- cultural and factional ownership  
- identity and augmentation linkage  
- era‑dependent behavior  
- quest and scene integration  

This is the **object‑level backbone** of myth and cosmic symbolism.

---

## 2. Core abstractions  

### **2.1 Artifact Seed**  
An Artifact Seed is the origin of a mythic object:

- a legendary event  
- a Void‑Class encounter  
- a cultural ritual  
- an augmentation breakthrough  
- a prophecy fulfillment or failure  

Each seed contains:

- origin myth  
- cultural lens  
- cosmic resonance profile  
- continuity constraints  

### **2.2 Resonant Core**  
The Resonant Core is the artifact’s metaphysical heart:

- **Resonance Type** — harmonic, drift‑bound, Void‑touched  
- **Intensity Level** — low, medium, high, volatile  
- **Cosmic Alignment** — era‑bound, culture‑bound, faction‑bound  
- **Identity Link** — sleeve‑bound, lineage‑bound, role‑bound  

### **2.3 Artifact Thread**  
Artifact Threads are long‑term narrative roles:

- protector artifact  
- destabilizer artifact  
- key artifact (unlocking, sealing, bridging)  
- witness artifact (recording, remembering)  
- anchor artifact (identity, continuity, culture)  

---

## 3. Artifact schema  

Each artifact includes:

- **Artifact ID**  
- **Name / Descriptor**  
- **Artifact Seed**  
- **Resonant Core Profile**  
- **Cultural Ownership / Contestation**  
- **Factional Claims**  
- **Era Binding**  
- **Mythic Narrative Role**  
- **Identity / Augmentation Link**  
- **Continuity Metadata**  

---

## 4. Artifact generation axes  

Artifacts evolve across **six axes**:

- **Symbolic Weight** — minor symbol ↔ central mythic pillar  
- **Cosmic Resonance** — low ↔ volatile  
- **Cultural Claim Strength** — shared ↔ fiercely owned  
- **Factional Instrumentalization** — ignored ↔ heavily weaponized  
- **Identity Binding** — generic ↔ lineage/sleeve‑locked  
- **Era Dependency** — timeless ↔ era‑specific

## 6. System behaviors  

### **6.1 Artifact Seed Detection**  
The system looks for:

- myth seeds from the Legend / Myth Engine  
- prophecy events from the Era‑Shift Prophecy Engine  
- Void‑Class encounters from the Cosmic Interaction Model  
- cultural rituals from the Cultural Narrative Weave  
- augmentation breakthroughs from Sleeve Architecture  

### **6.2 Resonant Core Construction**  
Cores are built from:

- cosmology laws (harmonics, drift, folds)  
- cosmic era context  
- mythic symbolism  
- identity and augmentation constraints  

### **6.3 Narrative Role Assignment**  
Artifacts are assigned roles:

- quest focus  
- scene catalyst  
- faction bargaining chip  
- cultural sacred object  
- identity anchor or destabilizer  

### **6.4 Era & Culture Interaction**  
Artifacts behave differently across:

- eras (active, dormant, inverted)  
- cultures (sacred, taboo, utilitarian)  
- factions (weapon, relic, bargaining token)  

### **6.5 Continuity Validation**  
The Narrative Continuity Engine ensures:

- artifacts remain canon‑consistent  
- resonance behavior obeys cosmology  
- identity binding stays within safety rails  
- cultural and factional claims don’t break coherence

## 7. Cross‑module integration  

- **Cosmology Engine / Cosmic Era Generator:**  
  - define resonance and era‑binding.  

- **Cosmic Interaction Model / Era‑Shift Prophecy Engine:**  
  - provide events that birth artifacts.  

- **Legend / Myth Engine / Cultural Narrative Weave:**  
  - define mythic meaning and cultural ownership.  

- **Faction Arc Generator / Adventure / Quest System:**  
  - use artifacts as stakes, keys, or catalysts.  

- **Scene Interaction Model / Interaction Style Codex:**  
  - shape how artifacts appear, are handled, and felt in scenes.  

- **Sleeve Architecture Spec:**  
  - govern identity and augmentation binding.

---

## 8. Safety & boundaries  

The Mythic Artifact System:

- keeps all artifacts fictional and symbolic  
- avoids mapping to real‑world religious or cultural objects  
- respects identity and augmentation safety rails  
- maintains narrative coherence  
- ensures artifacts are narrative tools, not prescriptive symbols
